<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#17:2> <h> A first for Jamaica </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#18:2> FOR the first time in Jamaica, a commercial bank has entered into

a joint venture agreement with an off <?> - </?> shore information processing

company to provide a new skill to be transferred to many Jamaicans. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#19:2> Through Computer Services and Programming Limited-CSP, Mutual

Security Bank has joined forces with National Transcription

Technologies-United States, to establish a medical transcription service

bureau that will provide services initially for NTT's North American clients.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#20:2> The company will be known as NTT ( Jamaica) Limited, and it will

operate out of the Free Zone at Fiscal Services, 234B Old Hope Road.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#21:2> The Jamaica dollar equivalent of US

$1.5 million will be invested by Mutual Security Bank over a two year period

while NTT ( US) has invested US$4 million. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#22:2> The announcement was made by Douglas Folkes, Managing Director of

Mutual Security Bank Limited and Chairman of Computer Service and Programming

at a press conference to announce the joint venture agreement between

Computer Service and Programming and National Transcription Technologies at

Mutual Security Bank's Board Room yesterday. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#23:2> He said the entity in the first three years would be owned

equally by Mutual Security and NTT-US on the assumption that NTT performed

the services as expected but during the fourth to fifth year NTT's ownership

would be increased and by year five, Mutual Security Bank will own 28 per

cent of the business. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#24:2> Mr. Folkes explained that Mutual Security Bank has a financial

target and on the basis that healthy profits will be generated from the

venture, the bank was willing to settle for 28 per cent of the entity. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#25:2> NTT-Jamaica is expected to generate a conservative revenue during

the first three years but in the fifth year and onwards a net inflow of US$10

million is expected. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#26:2> <h> Severe shortage </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#27:2> Mr. Folkes said there was a severe shortage of medical

transcription technicians in the USA whose work involved transcribing <}> <->

patients</-><+><w>patients'</w></+></}> records for medical practitioners.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#28:2> The US market for medical transcription, he said, was estimated to be

between US$600 million and US$1 billion annually. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#29:2> NTT has largely concentrated its efforts in the Florida area,

where there are about 220 hospitals and with this client base Mutual Security

was confident that in allying its computer service subsidiary with NTT it had

made a wise decision, he said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#30:2> Mr. Folkes said in addition to the direct returns from the

investment, CSP will be able to earn income from offsite data storage.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#31:2> This will be possible because of the level of compatibility of the

transmission lines and computer equipment in Jamaica with those in the United

States. </p>

<p> <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#32:2> " Another important benefit is training for Jamaicans in medical

information technology.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#33:2> At present, the profession of medical information technician does not

exist in Jamaica.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#34:2> NTT has been engaged with JAMPRO to address this vacuum," </quote> he

said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#35:2> Mr. Folkes said the aim was to establish the necessary knowledge

base to support an industry which gave indications of growth and so a

programme has been set up in collaboration with EXED Community College to

provide the required training, with NTT acting as a developmental and

monitoring consultant. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#36:2> Already the first batch of students has graduated from the

6-month training programme and was undergoing hands on training.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#37:2> The company NTT ( Jamaica) expects to employ 20 entry level

technicians after this first pilot training project but the number is <}> <->

excepted </-> <+> expected </+> </}> to exceed 350 in the fifth year of the

project. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#38:2> He said NTT ( Jamaica) will not only contribute significantly to

Jamaica's balance of trade over time but it will also provide lucrative

employment opportunities, and establish an educational base for a new

profession by providing a licensing programme. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#39:2> This will, hopefully, pave the way for other medical services

which are seeking cost effective labour solutions. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#40:3> <h> Govt hands over Printing Office </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#41:3> AN interim agreement to officially hand over operations of the

former Government Printing Office ( GPO) to Jamaica Printing Services ( 1992)

Limited ( JPS), the new company established by the former GPO workers, was

signed on Thursday by Minister of the Public Service, Dr. Paul Robertson, and

the Hon. Williams Isaacs, who is chairman of the new company. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#42:3> At the signing, which took place at the Oceana Hotel, Dr.

Robertson said that <quote> " under the agreement the former GPO premises

will be leased to the Jamaica Printing Services Limited for an initial period

of one year.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#43:3> And the company has agreed to relocate its operations during this

contract period." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#44:3> All equipment, furniture, fixtures and motor vehicles used in the

operations of the former GPO will also be leased to the new company.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#45:3> The JPS will continue negotiations with the Government and the

National Investment Bank of Jamaica for a long-term agreement. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#46:3> Having identified the capacity of the new company to handle the

printing needs of the Government, Dr. Robertson announced that the Jamaica

Printing Services Limited has been appointed as the Government Printer

commencing on August 15. </p>

<p> <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#47:3> " As Government Printer, the JPS will be authorized to print, publish

and distribute the Jamaica Gazette, Gazette Supplements and Gazettes

Extraordinary, according to the Jamaica Gazette Act.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#48:3> The company will also provide printing services to Parliament,

including the printing of Hansard, the Laws of Jamaica, all subsidiary

legislation, as well as other services," </quote> the Minister said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#49:3> He congratulated the former GPO workers, who have committed

approximately

$700,000 of their funds to capitalize the JPS, under the auspices of the

Employee Share Ownership Plan ( ESOP).
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#50:3> This move, he noted, was a positive one, which he hoped will receive

the full support of all other clients of the GPO. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#51:3> Speaking on the behalf of former GPO workers, Mr. Desmond Tucker,

a board member of Jamaica Printing Services Limited, said <quote> " the

employee owners of the company are committed to making the venture a

success." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#52:3> The former GPO production supervisor said that when the

Government Printing Office was closed in June, <quote> " we saw the scope for

operating the entity as a private employee <?> - </?> owned venture, and had

discussions with our unions with the aim of making worker ownership of the

GPO a reality." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#53:3> The Government Printing Office was closed on June 8 of this year

as part of the Government's move to rationalize the Public Service and

transfer certain public entities to private sector ownership.
<ICE-JA:W2C-020#54:3> At the time of the closure, employees of the GPO were given first

option to purchase the operations under ESOP. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-020#55:3> Through consultations with the National Investment Bank of

Jamaica, the workers indicated their acceptance of the offer and put in place

a team of employee representatives, lawyers, accountants and management

consultants to develop a business plan, and to conduct negotiations for the

acquisition of the GPO operations. </p> </I>
