Lagos to Get 30m Gallons More of Water
Written by Adeleke Afolayan
Lagos State according to Governor Babatunde Fashola is set to 
    generate an additional 30m gallons of water with the near 
    completion of 15 micro water 
    works to complement the 70m gallons per day being produced 
    by the Adiyan and Iju water 
    works.
Fashola made this known while receiving the Agric and Water 
    Resources Minister Dr. Abba Sayyadi Ruma, adding that an 
    agreement to generate 12 
    MegaWatts in its Independent Power Project has been signed 
    to power the water works in 
    Adiyan by the end of the year.
He also sought the partnership of the Federal Government and the 
    Ministry of Water Resources in the funding of a major water project 
    in the Epe area having successfully tackled the ocean surge problem 
    of the Lagos Bar Beach shoreline.
Speaking also at the 19th meeting of the National Council on 
    Water Resources, Fashola called for lucid policies that would 
    enable financial institutions to invest in the agriculture 
    sector.
According to him, it is strange that despite the 
    government's heavy investment in the sector, banks were still 
    reluctant to pump money in noting that clear-cut policies would 
    leave the banks with no choice but to invest in the sector.
