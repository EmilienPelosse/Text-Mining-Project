<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#6:1> <bold> RE<space> : <space> RESULTS OF Y2K COMPUTER

TESTING</bold></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#7:1> Earlier in the year, computers on the Mona campus were put through

a series of tests to determine their ability to handle problems associated

with the year 2000.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#8:1> The results of the tests on the machines in your department are

provided on the attached Compliance Report.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#9:1> Recognizing that the results of the tests may be <quote>

'confusing'</quote> to some, this document seeks to explain what the results

of the tests mean.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#10:1> <h> <bold> <ul> Understanding the Test

Results</ul></bold></h></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#11:1> The result of each test can be either a <quote> '1'</quote> or a

<quote> '0'</quote>.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#12:1> A <quote> '1'</quote> signifies that the test was <quote>

'passed'</quote> while a <quote> '0'</quote> signifies that the test was

<quote> 'failed'</quote></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#13:1> The following tests were performed on the machines:</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#14:1> &bullet; Ability to handle dates up to and beyond 2000</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#15:1> &bullet; Recognition of the year 2000 as a leap year and leap

years following</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#16:1> &bullet; Ability of the computers systems clock to <quote>

'rollover'</quote> from 1999 to 2000</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#17:1> &bullet; Testing of the computers <quote> 'real time

clock'</quote><fnr><sp>1</sp></fnr> via the BIOS ( Basic Input Output System)

<footnote>
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#18:1> <sp> 1</sp>Every personal computer contains two clocks  a built-in

real time clock ( RTC-also known as CMOS/RTC Chip) which is part of PC

motherboard and a system clock ( a virtual clock).
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#19:1> The RTC runs whether the system is on or off as long as the internal

battery is working.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#20:1> The system clock is set to the RTC when the computer is turned on and

exists only while the computer is operating.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#21:1> While the computer is up and running, the two clocks run independent

of each other.</footnote></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#22:1> &bullet; Testing of the computers <quote> 'real time

clock'</quote> via the CMOS ( Complementary Metal-Oxide Semiconductor)</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#23:1> Based on the results each machine was assigned a <bold>

Compliance Status Code.</bold>
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#24:1> These codes are set out below:</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#25:1> 0  Has no date function.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#26:1> Compliance is not an issue.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#27:1> 1  Y2K Compliant.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#28:1> 2  Can be made compliant with intervention such as user action,

software or hardware upgrade. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#29:1> 3  Product is being investigated, information not yet

available.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#30:1> 4  Cannot be effectively made compliant, seek alternative.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#31:1> 5  According to the manufacturer, product has been retired or

will be retired.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#32:1> Y2K compliance will not be assessed.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#33:1> The columns <bold> Date Setting, System Level, Bios Level</bold>

and <bold> CMOS Level</bold> on the Compliance Report represent summaries of

the tests performed.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#34:1> If the machine passes the <bold> CMOS Level</bold> tests then all

others should also have been passed and the machine is fully compliant.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#35:1> No action is necessary.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#36:1> Where a machine's <bold> Compliance Status Code</bold> is <quote>

2</quote>, one of the following actions may be necessary:</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#37:1> ( a) If the machine passes at the <bold> BIOS Level</bold> then

compliance can be assured by simply making sure that the machine is <bold>

OFF</bold> during the changeover period and rebooting after midnight December

31, 1999.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#38:1> Simply put, if the machine is not left running during the changeover

period it will be fine.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#39:1> ( b) If the machine passes the <bold> Date Setting</bold> and

<bold> System Level</bold> tests it should be possible to manually make the

machine compliant after Midnight 31st December 1999.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#40:1> It will be necessary to bring the machine up in <bold> DOS

MODE</bold> ( where you have the <quote> C:</quote> prompt showing) and set

the date there.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#41:1> This can be done as follows:</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#42:1> 1.) Type <quote> Date</quote> at the <quote> 'C'</quote> prompt

and press <quote> 'Enter'</quote></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#43:1> 2.) At the resulting prompt enter the date according to the

prompt shown.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#44:1> Some computers will prompt for a month/day/year entry while others

will prompt for a day/month/year entry.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#45:1> The year must be entered as a four ( 4) digit entry regardless of the

prompt.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#46:1> <bold> E.g.</bold> Prompt:<space> Enter new date ( mm-dd-yy):</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#47:1> <bold> You enter:</bold><space> 01-01-2000</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#48:1> It should not be necessary for you to do this again.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#49:1> In the event that there is any trouble getting this exercise

completed, personnel will be available to assist.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#50:1> A request must be made MISU to arrange to have someone on hand.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#51:1> If your machine has a status code <quote> '4'</quote> or <quote>

'5'<quote> it needs to be replaced before the year 2000.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#52:1> <bold> All machines being used for research must have a

compliance code of <quote> '1'</quote>.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#53:1> <h> <ul> What You Need To Do</ul></bold></h></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#54:1> Departments must confirm whether all machines in your department

are included on the Compliance Report list supplied, and send a reply in this

regard to the Y2K Committee, attention <@> Amy Richardson</@>.
<ICE-JA:W1B-019#55:1> Responses must be submitted by Wednesday, June 9, 1999.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-019#56:1> Departments may also direct any queries on the foregoing to the

Y2K Committee.</p></I>
