<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#1:1> <h> Alligator Pear Relish </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#2:1> Lady Nugent, wife of General George Nugent, Governor of Jamaica

from 1802-5, refers to avocado as <quote> 'subaltern's butter' </quote> in

her Journal.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#3:1> The impecunious junior officers of the British Army, stationed in

Jamaica, would use this cheap fruit to spread on their bread instead of using

butter, which was expensive and difficult to keep in the tropical Jamaican

climate prior to refrigeration. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#4:1> 1 Ripe avocado 1 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#5:1> 1 tbsp Lime or lemon juice 15 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#6:1> 1/4 tsp Salt 1 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#7:1> 1/8 tsp Freshly ground black pepper 5 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#8:1> 1 tbsp Busha Browne's Original Spicy Planters Sauce 15 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#9:1> Dash Busha Browne's Pukka Hot Pepper Sauce Dash </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#10:1> Crush avocado lightly and immediately sprinkle with lime or lemon

juice to prevent a change of colour.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#11:1> Add the salt, pepper, Planters Sauce and Pukka Sauce.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#12:1> Mix lightly with fork.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#13:1> Serve at once with breadfruit chips, pita bread triangles or crisp

crackers or toast. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#14:1> <smallcaps> Variation </smallcaps> :
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#15:1> The addition of a small amount of minced black olives or finely

chopped smoked oysters or grated onion gives another dimension to this

relish. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#31:1> <h> Miniature Meat Quenelles </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#32:1> Makes about 175 bite-size quenelles.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#33:1> These can be made and shaped well ahead of party time. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#34:1> 3 lb Ground chuck or round steak 1.5 kg </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#35:1> 1/2 cup Grated carrots 125 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#36:1> 1/4 cup Soy sauce 60 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#37:1> 1/4 cup Busha Browne's Original Spicy Planters Sauce 60 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#38:1> 1/4 cup Busha Browne's Spicy Tomato Love-Apple Sauce 60 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#39:1> 2 tsp Busha Browne's Spicy Jerk Seasoning ( optional) 10 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#40:1> 2 Eggs, lightly beaten 2 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#41:1> 3/4 cup Water 180 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#42:1> 2 Cloves garlic, put through a press 2 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#43:1> 2 tsp Ground ginger 10 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#44:1> Salt and pepper to taste </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#45:1> In a large bowl, combine the two or ( three) Busha Browne's

Sauces, water, garlic and ginger and mix until blended.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#46:1> Add the ground meat, carrots and eggs, and blend lightly but

thoroughly.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#47:1> Using a teaspoon, lift out spoonfuls of meat mixture, shaping lightly

with dampened hands into round balls under 1 inch ( 2.4 cm) in diameter.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#48:1> At this stage, if making ahead of time, cover tightly with plastic

wrap and refrigerate.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#49:1> When ready to cook the quenelles, put them in a single layer on a

large baking sheet in a slow oven 300 &degree; F ( 150 &degree; C) and bake

uncovered for about 15 minutes, turning once.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#50:1> Serve a few at a time on toothpicks, keeping the rest warm in a low

oven.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#51:1> Serve with dip. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#52:1> <smallcaps> Variation </smallcaps> :
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#53:1> Fish, veal or chicken may be used instead of beef to make these

delicate little morsels. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#63:1> <h> Smoked Marlin in Aspic </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#64:1> Smoked Blue Marlin is Jamaica's answer to Smoked Salmon.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#65:1> Tag and Release tournaments are held each year specially aimed at

capturing these big game fish.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#66:1> Marlin caught by local fishermen are smoked and served as an hors

d'oeuvre, in salad or other dishes. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#67:1> 4 tbsp Gelatin, softened in 1 cup water 60 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#68:1> 3 cups Concentrated chicken stock 750 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#69:1> 1/2 cup Lime or lemon juice 125 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#70:1> 1/4 cup Busha Browne's Spicy & Hot Pepper Sherry 60 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#71:1> 1 cup Diced red and green sweet peppers 250 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#72:1> 1 cup Diced celery stalks 250 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#73:1> 3 cups Flaked smoked marlin 750 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#74:1> Extra virgin olive oil to brush mould. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#75:1> Sprinkle gelatin on cold water to dissolve.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#76:1> Add boiling hot chicken stock to gelatin and stir until completely

dissolved.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#77:1> Add lime juice and Pepper Sherry.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#78:1> Refrigerate and allow to partially set: add diced sweet pepper and

celery.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#79:1> Fold in flaked smoked marlin.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#80:1> Put into mould which has been brushed with olive oil and allow to set

firmly.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#81:1> Transfer from mould onto platter, garnish and serve immediately. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#83:1> Tryall Estate, an elegant hotel and villa resort with an

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#89:1> 1/4 tsp Salt 1 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#90:1> 2 tbsp Butter 30 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#99:1> <h> Stamp & Go </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#100:1> <h> ( Saltfish fritters) </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#101:1> Salted codfish fritters are prepared throughout the Caribbean.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#102:1> The Jamaican version is known as <quote> 'Stamp & Go' </quote> ,

allegedly derived from the order given by officers on the 18th century

sailing ships in the British Royal Navy when something had to be done in a

hurry. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#103:1> 1/2 lb Dried, salted codfish 225 g </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#104:1> 1 tsp Busha Browne's Spicy & Hot Pepper Sherry 5 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#105:1> 1 cup All purpose flour ( 4 ozs) 250 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#106:1> 1 tsp Baking powder 5 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#107:1> 1/4 tsp Salt 1 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#108:1> 1 Egg, lightly beaten 1 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#109:1> 3/4 cup Milk 180 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#110:1> 1 tbsp Butter, melted 15 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#111:1> 1 Onion, finely chopped 1 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#112:1> 1/2 tsp Busha Browne's Pukka Hot Pepper Sauce 2 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#113:1> 1 tbsp Busha Browne's Original Spicy Planters Sauce 15 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#114:1> Vegetable oil for deep frying. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#115:1> Soak codfish in water to tenderize and remove salt.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#116:1> Drain, rinse and place in a saucepan of cold water to cover; bring

to the boil.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#117:1> Simmer until tender.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#118:1> Drain fish, remove bones and skin, and shred finely. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#119:1> Sift the flour, baking powder and salt together.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#120:1> Combine the egg, milk, butter and Pepper Sherry and stir into the

dry ingredients.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#121:1> Add the fish, onion, the Pukka Sauce and the Planters Sauce and mix

well.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#122:1> Drop by the tablespoonful into hot oil 375 &degree; F ( 190 &degree;

C) on a frying thermometer and fry until golden brown. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#123:1> Mixture should be runny enough so that the fritters spread to

form thin lacy patterns, which cook quickly to crispness.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#124:1> Drain on paper towels and serve hot with a dip of Busha Browne's

Spicy Tomato Love-Apple Sauce or Spicy Cocktail Dip ( see below).
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#125:1> Makes about 24 crisp, lacy fritters. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#126:1> <h> Busha Browne's Spicy Cocktail Dip </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#127:1> Mix 1/4 cup each Busha Browne's Original Spicy Planters Sauce

and Busha Browne's Spicy Tomato Love-apple Sauce with a dash of Busha

Browne's Spicy and Hot Pepper Sherry to taste.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#128:1> Serve with Stamp & Go as a dip. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#129:1> <smallcaps> Variation </smallcaps> :
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#130:1> Add 1 cup ( 250 ml) sour cream, plain unsweetened yogurt or Busha

Browne's Jiffy Mayonnaise ( page 81). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#165:1> <h> Villa de la Vega Quiche Squares </h> / p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#200:1> <h> Busha Browne's Hearty Red Pea Soup </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#201:1> 2 cups Red peas ( red kidney beans) 500 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#202:1> 2 qt Cold water 2 l </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#203:1> 1 1/2 cups Fresh coconut milk ( optional  see below) 375 ml

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#204:1> 1 lb Soup meat ( shin is best) 500 g </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#205:1> 4 Rashers bacon, or a salted pig's tail ( optional) 4 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#206:1> 1 tsp Busha Browne's Pukka Hot Pepper Sauce 5 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#207:1> 1 Coco ( or 1 Irish potato)  sliced 1 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#208:1> 1 Chopped onion 1 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#209:1> 1 Sprig fresh thyme 1 </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#210:1> 1 tbsp Busha Browne's Original Spicy Planters Sauce 15 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#211:1> 1 tbsp Busha Browne's Spicy Tomato Love-Apple Sauce 15 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#212:1> Salt and pepper to taste </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#213:1> 1/4 cup Busha Browne's Spicy & Hot Pepper Sherry 60 ml </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-012#214:1> Put peas to soak overnight.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#215:1> The following morning, drain the peas and discard water in which

they were soaked.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#216:1> Put the 2 qt ( 2 l) of water and the coconut milk into a large

saucepan.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#217:1> Add the meats and peas and the Pukka Sauce.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#218:1> Boil until peas are almost tender.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#219:1> Add coco ( or potato) and seasonings, including Planters Sauce and

Spicy Tomato Sauce.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#220:1> When peas and coco are cooked, remove the meats.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#221:1> Blend the liquid, the peas and the coco together, strain out the pea

skins and discard; or put through a colander and rub out.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#222:1> In either case discard the skins.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#223:1> A few whole peas may be retained to serve with the soup if desired.
<ICE-JA:W2D-012#224:1> Add Pepper Sherry. </p>
