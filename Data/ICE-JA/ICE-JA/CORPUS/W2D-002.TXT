<p> <h> <bold> Jamaican Standard for cosmetics </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#1:1> <h> Part 7: Method of test for the efficacy of antimicrobial </p>

<p> preservatives in water-based cosmetics </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#2:1> <h> 1. Scope </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#3:1> This standard specifies the test method used for determining the

suitability of preservatives for use in water-based cosmetics.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#4:1> It sets out minimum requirements for the performance of preservatives

in the cosmetics. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#5:1> <h> <bold> 2. Definition </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#6:1> For the purpose of this standard the following definitions apply:

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#7:1> <bold> 2.1 antimicrobial activity. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#8:1> The degree or extent to which the growth of microorganisms is reduced

or inhibited. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#9:1> <bold> 2.2 challenge organisms. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#10:1> The organisms used to test the ability of the preservative system to

inhibit the growth of microorganisms. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#11:1> <bold> 2.3 preservative. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#12:1> Any chemical or group of chemicals which is designed primarily to sup

<l> press the growth of microorganisms. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#13:1> <bold> 2.4 water-based cosmetics. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#14:1> Cosmetic products which have water as the main ingredient and the

emulsion is of the oil/water ( o/w) type. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#15:1> <h> <bold> 3. Summary of Method </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#16:1> The preservative system is challenged by inoculating the cosmetic

under test with known concentrations of different organisms.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#17:1> The microbiological test method outlined ( see clause <bold> 6

</bold> ) is used to determine the antimicrobiological activity ( see <bold>

2.1 </bold> ) of the preservative(s) used in the inoculation. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#18:1> <h> <bold> 4. Significance </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#19:1> This method should be used to determine if a chemical or group of

chemicals is applic <l> able as a cosmetic preservative. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#60:1> <h> <bold> 6. Procedure <bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#61:1> <h> <bold> 6.1 Sample preparation </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#62:1> <bold> 6.1.1 </bold> Transfer 100 mL/100 g portions of sample and

control sample to be tested, to sterile screw-capped bottles containing glass

beads ( for liquids) or sterile screw-capped wide-mouth jars ( for creams).
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#63:1> One sample container is required for each organism to be used in the

test. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#64:1> <typeface> NOTE.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#65:1> If possible, products should be inoculated in their original

containers. </typeface> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#66:1> <h> <bold> 6.2 Preparation of challenge cultures </bold> </h>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#67:1> <bold> 6.2.1 </bold> <it> Bacteria. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#68:1> Inoculate at least two agar slants ( nutrient agar or tryptic soy

agar) for each bacterial species to be used and incubate at 37 &degree; C for

18 h to 24 h.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#69:1> ( The transfers should be made from slants which are no more than 2

days old). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#70:1> <bold> 6.2.2 </bold> <it> Yeasts. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#71:1> Inoculate two agar slants ( Sabouraud dextrose agar) fro each yeast

species to be used.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#72:1> Incubate slants at 25 &degree; C for 48 h. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#73:1> <bold> 6.2.3 </bold> <it> Fungi. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#74:1> Inoculate three wide-mouth milk dilution bottle slants with 3

loopfuls of spores for each fungal species to be used.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#75:1> Incubate at 25 &degree; C for 7 days. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#76:1> <h> <bold> 6.3 Harvesting cultures </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#77:1> <bold> 6.3.1 </bold> <it> Bacterial cultures. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#78:1> Use a sterile inoculating loop to transfer the growth from each

culture into tubes of sterile distilled water or 0.1% peptone water.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#79:1> Alternatively, wash the growth from the slant with about 5 mL sterile

distilled water or 0.1% peptone water.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#80:1> Trans <l> fer the cell suspension to a sterile milk dilution bottle

containing 3 to 5 glass beads.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#81:1> Wash the same slant with a second portion of rinse liquid and

transfer to the bottles. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#82:1> Adjust the cell suspension to an optical density of 0.45 o.d.u.

in 12.7 mm diameter optical quality test tubes, using a spectrophotometer set

at 425 nm.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#83:1> This should give approximately 1.0 x 10 <sp> 8 </sp> bacteria/mL (

the cell suspension can be adjusted using serial dilution and pour plate

technique to get the desired concentrations of inocula). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#84:1> <bold> 6.3.2 </bold> <it> Yeast cultures. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#85:1> Harvest and adjust yeast cultures as described in <bold> 6.3.1.

</bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#86:1> This should give approximately 1.0 x 10 <sp> 7 </sp> yeast/mL. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#87:1> <h> <bold> 6.3.3 <it> Fungal cultures </it> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#88:1> <bold> 6.3.3.1 </bold> Pipette 5.0 mL of 0.1% peptone water into

a well sporulated slant of fungi.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#89:1> Shake gently to distribute the liquid over the entire surface to wet

the growth. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#90:2> <h> <bold> Jamaican Standard Specification for </p>

<p> Cosmetics: Part 8: Skin lotions and creams </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#91:2> <h> 1. Scope </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#92:2> This standard specifies the requirements for skin lotions and

creams.
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#93:2> It does not include products such as skin cleansing lotions and

creams which are intended to affect the structure and function of the skin.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#94:2> <h> <bold> 2. Definitions </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#95:2> For the purpose of this standard the following definitions apply:

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#96:2> <bold> 2.1. barrier agent. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#97:2> Any emollient agent which, in addition to promoting rehydration of

the skin, acts as protector of the skin surface. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#98:2> <bold> 2.2 emollient. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#99:2> Any agent which, when applied to dry or inflexible skin surface, will

effect a softening of that tissue by inducing rehydration as well as

protection. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#100:2> <bold> 2.3 film former. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#101:2> An agent used to impart a flexible film which provides a silky feel

as well as act as a protective barrier. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#102:2> <bold> 2.4 humectant. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#103:2> An agent that controls moisture exchange between the product and the

air, both in the jar and on the skin. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#104:2> <bold> 2.5 moisturizer. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#105:2> Any agent which when applied to dry or inflexible skin will regulate

the ability of the skin to retain moisture. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#106:2> <bold> 2.6 skin cream/lotion. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#107:2> Any oil-in-water ( o/w) emulsion preparation formulated for use in

the prevention and treatment of roughness and dryness of the skin. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#108:2> <bold> 2.7 thickener. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-002#109:2> An agent used for thickening or increasing the viscosity of the

product. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#110:2> <h> <bold> 3. Classification </bold> </h> </p>

following types: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#112:2> ( a)hand and body cream/lotion; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#113:2> ( b)facial cream/lotion. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#114:2> <h> <bold> 4. General requirements </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#115:2> <bold> 4.1 Description. </bold>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#119:2> The product shall be uniform in colour and have a pleasant

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#120:2> Lotions shall be fluid at ambient temperatures and shall have

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#126:2> ( d)leave no objectionable residue or film on the skin after 10

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-002#128:2> ( f)not interfere with normal skin perspiration; </p>
