<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-021#7:1> <h> <bold> INTRODUCTION </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-021#8:1> JAMAICAN bauxite and its process waste, red mud, are known to

contain levels of <sp> 238 </sp> U and <sp> 232 </sp> Th that are sig <l>

nificantly greater than those found in most other local soils.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#9:1> This was confirmed ( Pinnock and Graham 1988) in measurements that

were carried out as part of an effort to characterize the waste. One of the

potential uses of red mud noted in the characterization studies is its use in

constructing buildings.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#10:1> The material binds together strongly in its natural dry state, even

without the addition of binders, and can be used to produce a building block

that looks very similar to a traditional red clay brick.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#11:1> Questions about radiation hazards to building occupants have prompted

this study of the radiation characteristics of red mud when used as a

building material. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-021#12:1> Since it is the increment of dose above background that is the

appropriate basis for judging the hazard in <l> volved in using the material

( ICRP 1984), measurements are required of the activity present, not only in

red mud, but also in other materials that are normally used for con <l>

struction purposes.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#13:1> Activities of <sp> 226 </sp> Ra, <sp> 232 </sp> Th, and <sp> 40 </sp>

K form the primary basis for such a hazard assessment; thus, they were

determined in some relevant materials.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#14:1> This note describes the methodology and the results obtained. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-021#15:1> As a further step in evaluating the radiation hazard, effective

&gamma; dose equivalents were deduced from measure <l> ments made in a

prototype house constructed with red mud blocks.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#16:1> Dose-equivalent calculations were also made using the measured

radionuclide activities by employing model equations and appropriate values

for constants de <l> rived from the literature.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#17:1> This was done as a test for the usefulness of these model equations

when predicting doses, with a view to use such equations more generally in

deducing appropriate limits for radioactivity in building material.
<ICE-JA:W2A-021#18:1> The results of measurement and calculation are presented and

discussed below. </p>
