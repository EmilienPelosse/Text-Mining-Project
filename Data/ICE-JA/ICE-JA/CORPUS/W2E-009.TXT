<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#47:2> Newspapers <}> <-> moreso </-> <+> more so </+> </}> than any

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#49:2> I criticise journalists and columnists harshly when I think they

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#51:2> Columnists like Carl Wint and Raymond Forrest deserve special

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#52:2> Also in this category of highly informed commentary are the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#53:2> When informative comments are written, however, their value is

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#54:2> Wint's recent column using data drawn from a comparative

<ICE-JA:W2E-009#55:2> I could not agree with him more on our tendency to complain and

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#58:2> There is however, an important dimension of reality that Wint's

</}> life of all classes and especially among the poor experienced

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-009#62:2> For example in the late nineteen fifties when I left high school
