<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#1:1> <h> <bold> POSTGRADUATE PROGRAMME </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#2:1> The Department offers programmes leading to the M.A., M.Phil. and

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#8:1> &bullet; The Department will accept only that number of applicants

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#64:1> <h> <bold> M.PHIL. PROGRAMME </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#65:1> <bold> Entry Requirements: </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#66:1> An <it> Upper Second or First Class degree </it> is required, with

eight B+'s in History courses ( or in four year-long pre-semester courses).
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#67:1> In order to proceed from the M.A. to the M.Phil., students who do not

possess the normal qualifications for admission to the M.Phil. programme must

present an A grade research paper for the M.A. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#68:1> <bold> Programme: </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#69:1> M.Phil. students will be required to read three courses ( currently

H67L: Theory and Methods in History, H60A: The Emergence West Indian History,

and H60B: Historiography) for nine credits.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#70:1> For such students, examinations are held at the end of each semester

and students must pass ( 50% minimum) in order to proceed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#71:1> <it> The major component of the M.Phil. is the writing of an

extensive research thesis, <bold> working with a supervisor </bold> . </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#72:1> Topics may be chosen from the research areas listed or agreed by the

Department.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#73:1> <it> Students are required to meet with their supervisor are least

once per month. </it> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#74:1> <bold> Seminars: </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#75:1> All students are required to make <bold> at least <it> two </it>

presentations </bold> to the Departmental Seminar, one towards the end of the

first year of research and the other when the thesis is about to be written.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#76:1> Seminar papers must be circulated at least <it> seven </it> days

before the scheduled seminar.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#77:1> Graduate students are also <it> required </it> to attend a minimum of

50 percent of scheduled <bold> Departmental Seminars </bold> each year. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#78:1> <bold> Duration:
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#79:1> Full-time students </bold> can complete the M.Phil. programme in

<bold> two years </bold> . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#80:1> <bold> Examination: </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#81:1> The degree will be awarded after examination of the thesis and upon

the recommendation of the examiners to the Board of Graduate Studies and the

Senate.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#82:1> If the thesis is not acceptable, the candidate may be required to

re-submit within a specified time or may be deemed to have failed outright.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#83:1> <h> <bold> PH.D. PROGRAMME </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#84:1> <bold> Entry Requirements: </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#85:1> Admission to this programme is normally <it> via </it> the M.Phil.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#86:1> Students registered for the M.Phil. may in certain circumstances have

<ICE-JA:W2D-006#87:1> Such students must demonstrate an ability to cope with research at

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#89:1> <bold> Programme: </bold>

approved subject not less than two calendar years after direct admission to

the Ph.D. programme. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#91:1> The Ph.D. thesis must be a new and substantial contribution to

original research. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#92:1> The Ph.D. candidate must also take an oral examination following

submission of the thesis.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#93:1> This examination will cover their general field of study and the

before the scheduled seminar.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#97:1> Graduate students are also <it> required </it> to attend a minimum of

50 percent of scheduled <bold> Departmental Seminars </bold> each year. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#98:1> <bold> Duration:
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#99:1> Full-time students </bold> can complete Ph.D. programme in <bold> two

calendar years </bold> . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#100:1> <bold> Examination </bold> :
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#101:1> A candidate for the degree, having submitted the thesis and taken

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#132:2> <h> <bold> THE SOCIAL HISTORY PROJECT </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#133:2> The Social History Project of the Department of History, Mona,

was established in 1979, to encourage the study of West Indian social history

in the period since the abolition of slavery. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#134:2> Specifically, the Project attempts to achieve this end by: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#135:2> &bullet; Acting as a loosely institutionalized clearing house

for information on source materials, methods and comparative studies </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#136:2> &bullet; promoting the collection and preservation of social

documents and <}> <-> artifacts </-> <+> artefacts </+> </}> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#137:2> &bullet; directing research into areas of high priority on a

co-operative basis, and </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#138:2> &bullet; communicating findings to the public. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#139:2> The Social History Project is not meant to become a repository

for materials, but co-operates with existing institutions in the work of

collecting and preservation.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#140:2> The Project functions as a centralized guide to information.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#141:2> Initially, attention is being concentrated on Jamaica, but it is

hoped that a Caribbean-wide perspective can be achieved through comparative

work. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#142:2> The Project has so far been able to purchase microfilm copies of

the Jamaica <w> governors' </w> despatches for the period 1832-1880.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#143:2> These are available for use in the Main Library at Mona.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#144:2> The Project has also published a new edition of the Jamaica Censuses

for 1844 and 1861, and produced an index to a range of Jamaican periodicals

of the early 20th century.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#145:2> This index is available for use in the Department, and also in the

UWI Main Library and the National Library of Jamaica. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#146:2> The Project had the services of Research Fellows in Oral History

for 1989/90, 1991/92 and 1992/94.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#147:2> The Research Fellows have collected material on the history of

occupations in 20 <sp> th </sp> century Jamaica which is stored in the

Project Office. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-006#148:2> A <it> Newsletter </it> is published by the Project twice a

year, in June and December.
<ICE-JA:W2D-006#149:2> Anyone wishing to receive this <it> Newsletter </it> should contact

the Director. </p>
