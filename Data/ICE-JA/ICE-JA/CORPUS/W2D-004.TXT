<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#1:1> <h> <bold> 6. Sampling and sample handling </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#2:1> 6.1 Sampling. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#3:1> For specific sampling plans, refer to the standard for each product.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#4:1> <h> <bold> 6.2 Sample handling </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#5:1> 6.2.1 </bold> Samples shall be analysed as soon as possible after

arrival.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#6:1> If storage is necessary, samples shall be stored at room temperature

and shall not be incubated, refrigerated or frozen before or after analysis.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#7:1> <typeface> NOTE.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#8:1> Refer to standard specification for each product. </typeface> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#9:1> <bold> 6.2.2 </bold> Samples shall be inspected carefully before

opening and any irregularities of the sample container noted on the analyst's

worksheet. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#10:1> <bold> 6.2.3 </bold> The surface of the sample container shall be

disinfected with an aqueous mixture of 70% alcohol ( v/v) and the surface

dried with sterile gauze before opening and removing the con <l> tents. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#11:1> <bold> 6.2.4 </bold> If only one sample unit is available and

multiple analysis is requested, that is, micro <l> biological, toxicological,

chemical, the subsample for microbiological examination shall be taken prior

to those for other analyses.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#12:1> In this situation, the amount of the subsample used for microbial

analysis will depend on the other analyses to be performed.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#13:1> For example, if the total sample contents is 5 mL, a 1 mL or 2 mL

portion shall be used for microbiological analysis. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#14:1> <h> <bold> 7. Procedure </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#15:1> 7.1 Test portions. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#16:1> A representative portion of the contents shall be used for microbial

analy <l> sis.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#17:1> If possible a 10 g ( mL) sample shall be used.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#18:1> For products whose weight is less than 10 g ( mL) the entire contents

shall be analyzed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#19:1> <bold> 7.2 Preparation of dilutions. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#20:1> Irrespective of the type of cosmetic being analyzed prepare decimal

dilutions from 1:10 to 1:10 000 in diluent.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#21:1> Dilute decimally from 1:10 to 1:10 000 by adding 10 mL of a previous

dilution to 90 mL of diluent. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#22:1> <bold> 7.3 Preparation of samples. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#23:1> The types of equipment and supplies necessary for sample prepar <l>

ation and analysis will vary with the type of cosmetic preparation being

analysed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#24:1> <bold> 7.3.1 </bold> <it> Liquids. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#25:1> No special preparation of the sample is required.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#26:1> 10 mL of the sample ( if available) shall be directly added to 90 mL

of the diluent to obtain a 1:10 dilution.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#27:1> If this amount is not available, adjust the volume of diluent to

obtain an initial 1:10 dilution; for example 5 mL of sample added to 45 mL of

diluent. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#28:1> <bold> 7.3.2 </bold> <it> Solids and powders. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#29:1> Aseptically remove and weigh 1 g sample into 20 mm x 150 mm screw-cap

test tube containing 1 mL sterile Tween 80.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#30:1> Dispense product in Tween 80 with sterile spatula.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#31:1> Add 8 mL sterile <}> <-> modified </-> <+> modified </+> </}> letheen

broth ( see <bold> 5.3.3 </bold> ) and mix thoroughly.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#32:1> Add an appropriate amount of prepared sample to 90 mL <}> <->

modified </-> <+> modified </+> </}> letheen broth to obtain 1:10 dilution.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#33:1> Alternately aseptically weigh 10 g of the sample into 90 mL

diluent to obtain 1:10 dilution and mix thoroughly. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#34:1> <bold> 7.3.3 </bold> <it> Cream and oil-based products. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#35:1> Aseptically remove and weigh 1 g of sample into 20 mm x 150 mm

screw-cap tube containing 1 mL sterile Tween 80.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#36:1> Dispense product in Tween 80 with sterile spatula.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#37:1> Adjust total volume to 10 mL with sterile modified letheen broth, ( 8

mL broth) ( see <bold> 5.3.3 </bold> ) .
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#38:1> Add an appropriate amount of prepared sample to 90 mL modified

letheen broth to obtain 1:10 dilution.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#39:1> Alternate method as in <bold> 7.3.2. </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#40:1> <bold> 7.3.4 </bold> <it> Aerosols of powders, soaps, liquids and

other materials. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#41:1> After decontaminating the nozzle ( see <bold> 6.2.3 </bold> ) , expel

an appropriate amount of the product into a tared dilution or media bottle [

see <bold> 4.2 </bold> ( b)].
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#42:1> Mix thoroughly and reweigh the bottle. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#43:1> <h> <bold> 7.4 Microbiological evaluations </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#44:1> <h> 7.4.1 </bold> <it> Screening test for microbial contaminants

</it> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#45:1> <bold> 7.4.1.1 </bold> Inoculate 1 mL ( g) of sample directly

into a screw-cap bottle containing 90 mL modi <l> fied letheen broth, and

another 1 mL ( g) portion into a bottle containing 90 mL Sabouraud's dextrose

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#46:1> <bold> 7.4.1.2 </bold> If product is water-immiscible, for

before inoculating broths. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#47:1> <bold> 7.4.1.3 </bold> Incubate at 30 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C

for 7 days. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#48:1> <bold> 7.4.1.4 </bold> Confirm growth at 2 days and 7 days ( or

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#49:1> <bold> 7.4.1.5 </bold> Transfer 0.5 mL portion from Sabouraud's

dextrose broth to prepared malt extract or potato dextrose agar plate. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#50:1> <bold> 7.4.1.6 </bold> Transfer 0.5 mL from modified letheen

broth to prepared modified letheen agar plate. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#51:1> <bold> 7.4.1.7 </bold> Spread inoculum over surface of agar

plates, using sterile bent glass streaking rod. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#52:1> <bold> 7.4.1.8 </bold> Incubate 30 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#53:1> Record results of screening test. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#54:1> <h> <bold> 7.4.2 </bold> <it> Total aerobic plate count ( TAPC)

</it> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#55:1> <bold> 7.4.2.1 </bold> <it> Preparation of plates. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#56:1> Prepare and label duplicate petri dishes for the 1:10 to 1:10 000

dilutions.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#57:1> Thoroughly shake the dilution bottles 1:10 to 1:10 000 and transfer

using a sterile pipette 1 mL of each dilution into each of the appropriately

labelled duplicate petri dishes; or, using a sterile pipette transfer 0.5 mL

of each dilution to surface of prepared modified letheen agar or tryptic soy

agar ( see <bold> 5.2.29 </bold> ) , labelled duplicate plate.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#58:1> Add 20 mL to 25 mL melted tryptic soy agar TSA ( see <bold> 5.2.29

</bold> ) or letheen agar ( see <bold> 5.2.12 </bold> ) , tempered to 45

&degree; C, to each plate and rotate the plates thoroughly to mix the

inoculum. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#59:1> <it> Alternate method. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#60:1> Spread inoculum over entire surface of plate with bent glass rod that

has been sterilized in 95% ethanol and flamed to remove traces of ethanol.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#61:1> <bold> 7.4.2.2 </bold> <it> Incubation. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#62:1> Allow the agar to solidify or allow the spread inoculum to be

complete <l> ly absorbed by the agar.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#63:1> Invert the petri dishes and incubate at 35 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C

for 48 hours. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#64:1> <bold> 7.4.2.3 </bold> <it> Counting and recording colonies.

</it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#65:1> Count all colonies on plates containing 30 to 300 colonies and record

results per dilution counted.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#66:1> Count colonies after 24 h and 48 h incuba <l> tion.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#67:1> If plates do not contain 30 to 300 colonies, record dilutions counted

and note the number of colonies found. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#68:1> <bold> 7.4.2.4 </bold> <it> Reporting results. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#69:1> Average the counts obtained, multiply by the appropriate dilution

factor and report results as total aerobic plate count ( TAPC) per g ( mL)

sample. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#70:1> <h> <bold> 7.4.3 </bold> <it> Staphylococcus aureus ( S. aureus)

plate count </it> <h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#71:1> <bold> 7.4.3.1 </bold> <it> Inoculation. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#72:1> Transfer, using a sterile 1.0 mL pipette, 0.5 mL aliquots of the 1:10

dilu <l> tion to appropriately labelled duplicate plates of VJ agar ( see

<bold> 5.2.32 </bold> ) or MSA agar ( see <bold> 5.2.10 </bold> ) previously

tempered to room temperature.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#73:1> Distribute inoculum over the plate surface with a sterile bent glass

streaking rod [ see <bold> 4.1 </bold> ( g)]. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#74:1> <bold> 7.4.3.2 </bold> <it> Incubation. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#75:1> When the inoculum is completely absorbed by the agar invert the

plates and incubate at 35 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C for 24 h to 48 h. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#76:1> <bold> 7.4.3.3 </bold> <it> Typical colonies. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#77:1> Examine the plates for typical colonies of <it> Staphylococcus aureus

</it> .
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#78:1> Typical <it> Staphylococcus aureus </it> colonies appear on VJ afar

as convex, shiny black colonies with or without a yellow zone extending into

the medium.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#79:1> On mannitol salt agar ( MSA) ( see <bold> 5.2.10 </bold> ) typical

colonies of <it> Staphylococcus aureus </it> appear as cream-coloured

colonies with a yellow zone.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#80:1> From each plate showing typical colonies as described above, pick one

or more typical colonies and transfer individually to TSA ( see <bold> 5.2.29

</bold> ) , NA ( see <bold> 5.2.18 </bold> ) or PC agar ( see <bold> 5.2.21

</bold> ) maintenance slant.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#81:1> Incubate at 35 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C for 24 h to 48 h. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#82:1> <bold> 7.4.3.4 </bold> <it> Coagulase test. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#83:1> Inoculate a small amount of growth from the maintenance slant to 0.2

mL of brain heart infusion broth ( see <bold> 5.2.3 </bold> ) in a 13 mm x

100 mm test tube.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#84:1> Incubate for 18 h to 24 h at 35 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#85:1> Add 0.5 mL of reconstituted rabbit coagulase plasma with EDTA and mix

thoroughly.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#86:1> Incubate at 35 &plus-minus; 2 &degree; C and examine for clotting at

hourly intervals from one to six hours. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#87:1> All cultures giving a positive coagulase reaction in six hours

shall be considered as <it> Staphylo <l> coccus aureus </it> .
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#88:1> With every set of test samples include a known coagulase-positive and

coagulase-negative control. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#89:1> <bold> 7.4.3.5 </bold> <it> Reporting results. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#90:1> Calculate the number of <it> Staphylococcus aureus </it> by

determining the fraction of colonies tested that are coagulase-positive.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#91:1> Multiply this fraction by the average number of typical colonies

appearing on the duplicate plates.
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#92:1> Multiply by the appropriate dilution factor and report as the number

of <it> Staphylococcus aureus </it> per gram ( mL of sample <+> ) </+> .
<ICE-JA:W2D-004#93:1> If no typical colonies are found, report the result as <quote> 'no

<it> Staphylococcus aureus </it> found per gram ( mL)' </quote> . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-004#105:1> <h> <bold> 7.4.5 </bold> <it> Screening test for anaerobic
