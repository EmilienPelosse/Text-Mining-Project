<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#37:2> <h> <bold> Re:<space> Public Statements and Research

Ethics</bold></h></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#38:2> As some of you may be aware the University of the West Indies has

an established policy on research ethics which deals with issues of giving

accurate and appropriate credit for work done, falsification of data and

plagiarism, dispute resolution and a number of other matters affecting the

conduct of researchers, whether Faculty or student.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#39:2> A copy of the document is attached to this memorandum for easy

reference.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#40:2> Please familiarize yourself with its contents.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#41:2> An aspect of this matter which I wish to bring to your urgent

attention is that of public statements and publications which are issued by

University agencies, Faculty or students giving information about research

projects and related activities taking place at the University to the general

public.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#42:2> It is of the utmost importance that any such information be accurate

and that great pains are taken to ensure that credit for work being done is

accurate, appropriate and complete.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#43:2> This is especially important in these times since issues of

intellectual property rights are invariably at stake and often also theses of

graduate students may be involved.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#44:2> Recent experience suggests that it is surprisingly easy for

errors, omissions, and ambiguous photographs of research activities to be

inadvertently published in the course of the laudable efforts to publicize

our work.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#45:2> We cannot be too careful on these matters as they are very difficult

to correct after the fact and can have very severe repercussions on us

all.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#46:2> I therefore take this opportunity to invite you to exercise the

utmost caution on these matters.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#47:2> Any intended publication on research activity must be carefully

vetted by the Faculty member(s) ( and graduate students where appropriate)

concerned.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#48:2> The parties directly involved should make a written confirmation to

the Public Relations ( or relevant office) that to the best of her/his

knowledge it is accurate, appropriate and complete.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#49:2> It is also highly advisable that copies of correspondence on these

issues be kept on file.
<ICE-JA:W1B-017#50:2> Directors and Heads of Departments have a special responsibility to

ensure that this is done.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#51:2> Thank you for your kind cooperation.</p></I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-017#152:8> Council is now asked to ratify this decision, while bearing in

the St. Augustine Campus.</p>
