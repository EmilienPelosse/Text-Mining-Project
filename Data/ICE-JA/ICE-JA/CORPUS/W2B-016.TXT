<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X49:1> At this time Norman Manley was at the height of his career; a

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X65:1> Jamaica Welfare Limited was registered on 7 June, 1937, with the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X67:1> To engage in any work or activity directly or indirectly

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X68:1> The Directors were Ansell Hart, Lewis Ashenheim, U Theo McKay,

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X80:1> The decision was taken to establish Community Centres which

<ICE-JA:W2B-016#X83:1> Evan Donaldson, who had been an associate of Hallett in voluntary

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X84:1> In August of the following year, a second Centre was opened in

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X98:1> Manley was convinced that cooperatives had great potential for

<ICE-JA:W2B-016#X99:1> D T M Girvan, recruited from a promising business career, was

<ICE-JA:W2B-016#X101:1> The Jamaica Cooperative Union was formed in 1942 to promote the

<ICE-JA:W2B-016#X102:1> The principal officers of the Union were Thom Girvan, Chairman;

<ICE-JA:W2B-016#X103:1> The Jamaica Cooperative Development Council was also formed to

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-016#X105:1> It had been hoped that the activities at the Community Centres

<ICE-JA:W2B-016#X112:1> A Village Committee would be formed of a few interested persons who
