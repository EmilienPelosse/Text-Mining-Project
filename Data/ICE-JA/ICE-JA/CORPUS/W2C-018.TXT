<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#41:2> <h> US group backs CBI countries </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#42:2> JAMAICA's position that Caribbean Basin Initiative ( CBI)

countries should be accorded similar treatment to those NAFTA signatories are

to get has the backing of the powerful and highly influential United States

Apparel Industry Council ( USAIC). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#43:2> The Council yesterday issued a press release urging that <quote>

" immediate parity" </quote> be provided to CBI countries for <quote> "

preferences given to Mexico in relation to trade in textiles and apparel

under the NAFTA". </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#44:2> In welcoming the conclusion of the North American Free Trade

Agreement ( NAFTA) between United States, Canada and Mexico, which makes the

360-million,

$6 trillion-strong group the largest trading bloc in the world, the USAIC

sounded the warning that <quote> " failure to provide parity will cause CBI

countries to lose current investment and investment opportunities." </quote>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#45:2> The release went on to stress the point that <quote> " US apparel

company investments in the CBI region will be jeopardised if immediate parity

is not provided." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#46:2> The statement from this highly influential US apparel lobby group

represents a boost to the position of Caribbean Basin apparel interests.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#47:2> Jamaica's Chief Trade Representative and JAMPRO Vice President for

International Marketing and Trade, Ambassador Peter King, has been a main

spokesman for CBI apparel interests and has done considerable lobbying in

mentioned by the USAIC. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#48:2> Delighted that his lobbying work has borne fruit, he said he was

pleased at the goodwill and support expressed by the statement of the USAIC.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#49:2> On Thursday morning, Ambassador King received the Mexican

Ambassador to Jamaica, Sr. Luis Ortiz Monasterio.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#50:2> The two ambassadors discussed the recently concluded NAFTA Agreement

and emphasized that there were some positives which could accrue from it.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#51:2> It was pointed out that Mexico had become a more important trading

partner and Jamaican business interests should now turn their attention to

exploiting the opportunities on the Mexican market.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#52:2> To further this objective, a decision was taken to open a Mexican

interest section in the JAMPRO Library at 35 Trafalgar Road, to facilitate

<p> <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#53:2> " An information network is vital to any expansion of trade or

<ICE-JA:W2C-018#54:2> We need information on shipping, channels of distribution, prices,

<ICE-JA:W2C-018#55:2> All this will be provided in the Mexican section of JAMPRO's Library,

Mexico will do the same through its counterpart to JAMPRO. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#56:2> Mexico is now the second largest trading partner with the United

<ICE-JA:W2C-018#57:2> " This represents a major opportunity for us," </quote> says the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#59:3> <h> Tourism poised for major expansion </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#60:3> By Margaret Morris </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#61:3> JAMAICA faces a huge expansion of tourist accommodation that will

add a further 3,000 rooms. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#62:3> There is now a list of at least 10 hotels or condo projects and

this does not include planned additions to existing properties of elusive

dreams like the Rollins Rosehall project. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#63:3> The list includes the high-profile Butch Stewart Beaches project,

the low-profile Couples Negril and Couples MoBay, the controversial

Melhado-Cameron development in Negril and the Round Hill Hyatt project. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#64:3> Round Hill Hyatt: Informed Montego Bay sources say that plans are

well advanced for the 600-acre prestige site and a US$150 million project,

including a 300-room hotel, townhouses and villas, and a golf course.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#65:3> Work permits have been requested for the expatriate contractor,

engineers and architects and shipping containers have been booked to

transport necessary equipment. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#66:3> Agreement in principle has been granted for the relocation of the

main road. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#67:3> The foreign developers are represented by Pino Maffessanti and

Hyatt will be operators.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#68:3> Pino Maffesanti is off the island and his son Mark told the <it>

Gleaner </it> that they are major shareholders of Round Hill Development Co.

Ltd. which owns the land but he <quote> " knows nothing" </quote> of any

projected hotel plan and <quote> " nobody has made us an offer" </quote> for

the site. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#69:3> Couples: The 250-room Couples Negril on Bloody Bay may open for

the 1994-95 winter season.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#70:3> LOJ investment manager Roger Brown told the <it> Gleaner </it> that

it will be a 50-50 joint venture between LOJ/House of Issa in respect of the

property and the operating company, and Issa will manage it. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#71:3> <h> No longer involved </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#72:3> Plans for the 240-room Couples MoBay at Mahoe Bay appear less

firm although the Commonwealth Development Corporation and International

Finance Corporation have already committed US$14 million to the US$28 million

project.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#73:3> A spokesperson for Mahoe Bay Co. Ltd. ( a Canadian company registered

in Jamaica) told the <it> Gleaner </it> that the company will supply the

necessary local funding making it a full joint venture between Mahoe Bay Co.

and House of Issa. Major shareholder of Mahoe Bay is Winston Finzi.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#74:3> The <it> Gleaner </it> understands the original local backer, Workers

Bank, is no longer involved.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#75:3> Mr. John Issa did not return this reporter's telephone queries. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#76:3> Projects which have already received preliminary planning

permission include Beaches in Westmoreland, Braco in Trelawny, Pear Tree

Bottom in St. Ann and Drax Hall in St. Ann. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#77:3> Projects of UDC land which do not require standard approvals

include the Melhado- Cameron hotel in Negril, the Richfield ( Panton)

complex planned by the ISSAR company owned by the Khemlanis.
<ICE-JA:W2C-018#78:3> All these have alarmed the environmental lobby and sparked

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#79:3> Other large projects still in the formative stage include the

to the proposed site of Beaches and a Pan-Jam development at Florida Beach

near Falmouth. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-018#80:3> A conservative estimate of the tourist rooms to be added by all

these developments and by extensions of existing properties, for example

Cibonev, is close to 3,000. </p> </I>
