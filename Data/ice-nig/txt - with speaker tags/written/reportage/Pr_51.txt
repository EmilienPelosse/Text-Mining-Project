Lack of quality friendly games frustrates Siaisia
National Under-20 Coach Samson Siasia has expressed frustration 
    at the inability of his boys to play friendly matches which are up 
    to standard in preparation for the Under-20 World Cup which will 
    hold in Egypt in September.
Siasia stated that he was disappointed he will not be playing 
    the Young Pharaohs of Egypt before the World Cup.
He said: "I don’t even know what is going on. But I am very 
    disappointed. I was actually looking forward to playing friendly 
    games with good football teams. For instance that of Malaysia was 
    because of the swine flu .
"But when we go to Qatar and come back we will arrange a 
    game between Ghana or Ivory Coast. We need to play games not just 
    any game at all. Hw do you access 
    these players without playing a good game? So it’s going to be very 
    difficult. We will come back from Qatar and we will try to play 
    some friendly matches before we go to Spain."
The Flying Eagles who won last year's Intercontinental Cup 
    in Malaysia, have continued to experienced hiccups in their various 
    attempts which include the assembling of players.
On June 23 the Flying Eagles will take on the home-based Super 
    Eagles once again in friendly. It could be recalled that the 
    home-based Eagles defeated the Siaisa side by 1-0 few weeks ago.
Meanwhile, the team is expected to go on a recess for ten days 
    after which they are expected to assemble back for a training tour 
    to Qatar on the 4th of July.
Nigeria was drawn in group B alongside Spain, Venezuela and 
    Tahiti in the Under-20 World Cup to be hosted by Egypt.
