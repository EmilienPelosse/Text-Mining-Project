<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#1:1> <h> Man's face eaten by maggots </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#2:1> By Gary Spaulding </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#3:1> A MAN with the lower part of his face gone, consumed by thousands

of hungry maggots digging away into his flesh, was discovered on the premises

of the St. Elizabeth Church on Ransford Avenue in the Maxfield Avenue area,

yesterday. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#4:1> Totally emaciated and obviously in pain, the man was unable to

talk as his mouth is completely eaten away.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#5:1> He was taken to the Missionary of Charities, a home which accommodates

a number of elderly indigents on Tower Street. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#6:1> At the institution, seven people including five Nuns from India

who run the place assumed the evidently difficult task of tending to him.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#7:1> After working on him for more than two hours they began to express

fear that the insects may have reached his brain as they were up to that

point unsuccessful in getting rid of the maggots. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#8:1> A gruesome picture of the 'sisters' using tweezers to remove

maggots from the hole which was the lower part of the man's face greeted the

<it> Gleaner </it> team on its arrival at the home minutes past 2 p.m.

yesterday. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#9:1> His bared teeth and nose-less face gave him a frightening

appearance.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#10:1> He was given water and gulped wildly instead of drinking and grunted

when he attempted to talk. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#11:1> Pointing to a container of maggots which was earlier removed by

the application of <quote> 'gallons of antiseptic' </quote> the Nuns who said

they had been on the job for more than two hours and had used tweezers in an

attempt to budge the more stubborn ones. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#12:1> <h> Second time </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#13:1> An elderly woman told the <it> Gleaner </it> that earlier, when

he was brought to the home, his beard, long, filthy and knotty, was swarmed

with <quote> " millions of crawling maggots." </quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#14:1> She said the people who were assisting him had to scuttle out of the

way of <quote> " hundreds of flying maggots" </quote> when the man sneezed.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#15:1> This is the second time in a week that the institution had come

to the rescue of men plagued by maggots.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#16:1> Two days ago, a man was picked up downtown with his arms filled with

maggots.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#17:1> He was treated by the nuns and was lying in bed recuperating when the

<it> Gleaner </it> team visited. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#18:1> The nuns expressed surprise that the afflicted man appeared to be

sane.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#19:1> Making specific reference to the man they had picked up downtown one

of the nuns declared, <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#20:1> " They are surprisingly sharp.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#21:1> I wonder what went wrong." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#22:1> The old people had high praise for the nuns whom they said came

from abroad to work voluntarily but lamented the direction in which the

country was going when <quote> " people in this condition are allowed to

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#23:1> An angry woman who helps at the institution puts it this way:

<quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#24:1> " The tragedy is that thousands of people passed the ailing man by

<ICE-JA:W2C-013#25:1> It is an indictment against the Government and the people of the

country.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#26:1> And we talk about justice and peace be ours.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#27:1> It is a national disgrace." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#28:1> She also expressed concern over the functions of the Ministry of

<ICE-JA:W2C-013#29:1> " It is <-> a </-> really a matter of public health.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#30:1> Are the sick, whether old or young, to be allowed to be left

untreated on the highways when the public is burdened by heavy taxation?"

</quote> she asked. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#31:2> <h> New JTA president promises... </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#32:2> <h> <quote> 'Total transformation of the educational system'

</quote> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#33:2> By Mark Dawes </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#34:2> NEW Jamaica Teachers Association president, Ray Howell, has

promised to seek the <quote> " total transformation of the educational

system... as our progress as a nation can be no swifter than our progress in

education." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#35:2> Mr. Howell, 36, was speaking at an investiture ceremony held as

part of the 28th Annual General Conference of the Jamaica Teachers

Association which ends today at the American Beach Resort Hotel, Ocho Rios.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#36:2> The new president, who is also principal of the Drew's Avenue

Primary School, spoke on a number of issues, which included the Government's

failure to adequately remunerate teachers, the need for education to be seen

as a long-term investment, the use of the Education Tax, and the delay in

appointing teachers to senior posts that are vacant. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#37:2> In a speech punctuated by frequent applause, Mr. Howell said that

with the exception of those in Guyana the Jamaican teacher was the lowest

paid in the Caribbean.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#38:2> Other Caribbean teachers were getting between three to 20 times more

than the highest-paid teacher in Jamaica, he said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#39:2> Quality education, he said, would come about with the recruitment

and retention of quality teachers.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#40:2> <quote> " However, quality teachers do not come more cheaply than

quality doctors, business managers or lawyers." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#41:2> <h> Quality product </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#42:2> Mr. Howell said that quality education would come about <quote> "

when the conditions under which it may thrive are identified and carefully

nurtured and made available by those who hold power to assist in creating

those conditions.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#43:2> If you want a quality product then you are careful to monitor all the

stages through which it goes to ensure that the end result will be

satisfactory," </quote> he said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#44:2> Mr. Howell called on the Government to: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#45:2> &bullet; Use the

$428 million it collected from the Education Tax last year for the original

purpose; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#46:2> &bullet; Pay grants owing to Primary and All-Age schools since

April; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#47:2> &bullet; Facilitate school principals with the speed-up of the

process by which teachers are appointed to senior positions, particularly

whenever such positions are vacant. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#48:2> &bullet; Desist from granting building contracts for schools to

people who are incompetent in matters relating to construction. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#49:2> Immediate past president, Rosemarie Vernon, called for a <quote> "

fundamental change in the formal education system." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#50:2> These changes, she said, must include greater involvement of the

private sector in education; an increase in teacher remuneration to

adequately cover the cost of living; and the appointment of all educators on

the basis of qualification and not through political affiliation. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#51:2> The conference ends today.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#52:2> The highlight will be a meeting between Education Minister Burchell

Whiteman and the delegates. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#53:2> Among those in attendance yesterday were Mr. Burchell Whiteman;

Parliamentary Secretary in the Ministry, A.J. Nicholson; Opposition Spokesman

on Education, Dr. Neville Gallimore; the Anglican Bishop of Jamaica, Rt. Rev.

Neville DeSouza; and the Custos of St. Ann, Dr. Osmond Tomlinson. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#54:3> <h> Taxi operators setting their own fare rates </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#55:3> By Ann-Marie Mittoo </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#56:3> TAXI operators are no longer using the fare structure set by the

Transport Authority.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#57:3> This is because the rate was last revised in 1984 based on the

metering system. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#58:3> The operators say the minibus operators are getting an increase

in their fares and robot minibuses are quickly weeded out of the system yet

no similar attention is paid to them. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#59:3> This is the cry of many taxi operators who consider themselves to

be neglected by the Transport Authority, the governing body that regulates

their fare structures and licensing. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#60:3> According to Transport Authority Chairman Hensley Williams, there

are plans afoot to look more closely at the entire taxi operation.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#61:3> But this will not be done until the 147 buses are strategically

placed on routes. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#62:3> <quote> " We have to look closely at the entire operation in

terms of units in the system, fare structure and metering and try to come up

with a position," </quote> Mr. Williams said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#63:3> The Transport Chairman said a restructuring of routes was being

done to maximise efficiency on the routes. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#64:3> Most taxi companies are not members of any one umbrella

association and operate under their own policies.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#65:3> There is only one association formed since 1990, called Metropolitan

Taxi Owners Association.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#66:3> This association now has 53 individual members. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#67:3> <quote> " We cannot afford to go by the last fare structure and

so we have to set our own rates," </quote> said Milton Chung of Metropolitan

Taxi Owners Association. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#68:3> According to the last set of rates which applies to both metered

and unmetered taxis, fares begin at

$2.50 for the first one-third of a mile or part thereof; for each additional

one- third of a mile or part thereof it is 85 cents; for each waiting period

of four minutes it is also 85 cents.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#69:3> For a journey ending between 12 midnight and 5 a.m. an additional 25

per cent is charged. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#70:3> <quote> " The rates are unrealistic and not in keeping with

present economic conditions," </quote> one operator said. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#71:3> The metering system is almost obsolete.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#72:3> When the <it> Gleaner </it> checked with some taxi companies none of

them were using the meter system.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#73:3> Most of them did not have a meter in place.
<ICE-JA:W2C-013#74:3> However, Mr. Willliams said the law made provision for unmetered

taxis as well. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#75:3> The meters were phased out after the high cost in getting spare

parts, they could no longer calibrate at the changing fare stage and the

Government did not have the foreign exchange to purchase the meters which are

manufactured abroad. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#76:3> A spokesman with the former Jamaica Taxi Cab company said a flat

rate is being charged according to mileage. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-013#77:3> Taxi operator Raymond Grant said meters were too expensive but

cautions that passengers should ask the cost of fares before taking the

taxis. </p> </I>
