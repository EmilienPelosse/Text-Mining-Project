<p> <h> CHURCH AND STATE </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#1:1> By Martin Henry </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#2:1> IT is quite wrong to believe that the ecclesiastical structures of

Christianity have mostly been benefactors of society.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#3:1> The churches have more often than not stood on the side of injustice,

ignorance and oppression than they have stood against these indignities to

the spirit and ideals of man.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#4:1> This is a fact of history, and should be a cause of very great

embarrassment for the Christian religion at large as it is for this humble

believer. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#5:1> Where the churches have done 'good', to society in the liberal

democratic sense of the word, they have done so either as an outflow of

spasms of internal reformation and revival or as a belated response to social

revolutions which were launched by essentially secular men. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#6:1> Christianity has generally handled political power badly, using

the sword of the state as an instrument of coercion rather than as a tool of

liberation.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#7:1> This is not a matter of purely historical concern.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#8:1> The question of Church and State relationship is a living question of

the historic present.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#9:1> Historical analysis must therefore be called upon to provide rational

guidance on how to proceed in this delicate arena.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#10:1> It is extremely rash and foolish to discount the clear testimony of

history and to assume that in this enlightened present the churches cannot be

allied to the forces of injustice, ignorance and oppression as they were

repeatedly in the past. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#11:1> Let us draw lessons from our own West Indian history, before

going further afield:
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#12:1> There is no question at all that the established churches supported

slavery, and more than that, that they provided the theological justification

for the practice which began purely out of the economic 'necessity' for

labour in the colonies. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#13:1> <h> Fight against slavery </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#14:1> Much ado is made by the historians of the efforts of the

non-conformist missionaries in the fight against slavery and in the

establishment of a free peasantry.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#15:1> Credit must be given where credit is due.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#16:1> But we must accurately note that the philosophical roots of the anti

<?> - <?/> slavery movement originated in the Enlightenment with its lofty,

secular humanist views of the dignity of man, of liberty and of justice, and

its rejection of the static hierarchical system of social organization which

was supported by the state churches of Europe.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#17:1> The spirit of the Enlightenment infused the Christian reformation

movements of the time such as Methodism, led by the Wesley brothers, which

then fought for the ideals of humanism. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#18:1> The abolitionists were first and foremost humanists and

humanitarians and secondarily, for some of them like Wilberforce, Christians.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#19:1> Historian Eric Williams devoted a whole chapter of his book

Capitalism and Slavery to <quote> " The <quote> 'Saints' and Slavery"

</quote> , and makes the point: <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#20:1> " The humanitarians were the spearhead of the onslaught which

destroyed the West Indian System and freed the Negro" </quote> .
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#21:1> One of their number, James Stephen Jnr., wrote that, <quote> " the

ultimate end of human society - the security of life, property and reputation

- must be preferred to its subordinate ends - the enjoyment of particular

franchises" </quote> ( like owning slaves). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#22:1> Stephen's Colonial Office memorandum reads much like the

preambles of the American Declaration of Independence and Constitution which

were drawn up several years before by men who were essentially humanistic in

their outlook. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#23:1> The riddle of the differential prosperity of North America and

Latin America is best unlocked by examining differences in the historic power

of the churches on both sides of the Rio Grande. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#24:1> <h> Resources </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#25:1> A secular democratic constitution has underpinned the

construction of the greatest nation the world has yet seen, ( judged from the

vantage point of the underdog man).
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#26:1> While, with similar resources of land and people, its southern

neighbours have some of the greatest disparities, in land distribution, in

wealth, education, privilege, and power with the church being the largest

single landowner. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#27:1> Nor should we forget that the most powerful churchman of the time

of the formulation of the American constitution declared that that document -

the prototype charter of democratic freedoms everywhere - was full of <quote> "

pestilential errors" </quote> . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#28:1> We must of course also remember that, despite its magnificent

charter of freedom, the United States remained a slave holding state, with

church support, until 1863 when President Lincoln proclaimed Emancipation.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#29:1> And the Salem witch hunt of 1692 remains a major blot on the Human

Rights record of America, a country to which millions have fled to find

relief from religio-political persecution. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#30:1> It is ironic that the Latin American church has given rise to

Liberation Theology which seeks to fuse ecclesiastical power and state power

to socialism - a losing combination if ever there was one.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#31:1> Liberation Theology is neither good religion nor good politics. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#32:1> The Protestant Reformation lighted candles in a dark, static

Europe, dominated for a thousand years by the Church which assigned itself

the task to be the unquestioned conscience of all men who deviated from dogma

on pain of death.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#33:1> The Lion Handbook, 'The History of Christianity', claims that lasting

social, political and economic changes followed the Reformation and to some

extent shaped it. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#34:1> But the great religious wars of Europe followed, as the various

factions of a splintered church sought to dominate each other and to forcibly

control men, - body, mind and spirit - via the sword of the state.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#35:1> The great German reformer Martin Luther himself, stood against the

revolt of the peasants against harsh political and economic conditions heaped

upon them by monarchical rulers. </p>

<p> <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#36:1> " Better the death of all the peasants, than the princes," </quote>

Luther declared, <quote> " inasmuch as they are all evil-minded and brazenly

refuse to obey, and furthermore resist their masters." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#37:1> <h> Rebellious person </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#38:1> Luther further said, <quote> <X> " They have forfeited life and

soul as do all faithless perjured, mendacious, disobedient knaves and

villains. Therefore it becomes the duty of all here to strangle and stab

secretly or publicly, all such, and remember that there is nothing so

poisonous, injurious and fiendish as a rebellious person, just as you would

kill a mad dog: if you do not strike him he will strike you, and with you,

the whole country."</X></quote> The princes took the advice of their

spiritual mentor. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X39:1> Meanwhile, Calvin was busy in Geneva creating a <quote> " model

christian society" </quote> by the same combination of preaching and force.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X40:1> For more than 100 years no musical instrument was allowed in

Calvinist Geneva! </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X41:1> Modern liberal democratic societies confer political power upon

ecclesiastical authorities at their own risk.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X42:1> The track record of the church, with the sword of the state in its

hand, is not a good one.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X43:1> The distant historical examples cited can be more than matched by

recent contemporary ones. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X44:1> Historians tend to shy away from the known alliance of elements

of Christianity with Nazism and Fascism before and during the Second World

War, a theme which novelist and former Jesuit professor and close associate

of Pope John XXIII, Malachi Martin, explores in 'Vatican'.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X45:1> We are, however, in no doubt about the role of the Dutch Reformed

Church in supplying the theological and moral underpinnings for Apartheid up

until very recent times.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X46:1> And we should carefully watch the roles of resurgent ecclesiastical

powers in re-shaping post-Cold War Eastern Europe ( something which the News

either don't know how to, or won't report). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X47:1> In light of the near exhaustion of secular humanism, the

excesses of liberal democracy and the anarchising of freedom, the search is

on for a moral North Star to guide societies which are morally adrift.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X48:1> The separation of Church and State, which has given Western

Societies the greatest freedom and material prosperity ever known among

ordinary men ( even in Jamaica), is therefore up for review. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X49:2> <h> Elections and television </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X50:2> By Beth Aub </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X51:2> THE latest round of extensive cricket coverage by Jamaica

Broadcasting Corporation television has illustrated, once again, that Jamaica

MUST have at least one other television channel.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X52:2> Those people who do not care about, or are bored by the game of

cricket - and this, surely, includes most women and a fair portion of men -

were disgusted by the blocks of hours and hours of prime T.V. time which our

ONLY channel recently diverted to covering the matches in Australia. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X53:2> J.B.C. allowed cricket to push aside all other programmes,

except a number of those 'soaps' which promote the frivolous foreign values

that J.B.C. apparently considers essential to Jamaican life!
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X54:2> As a young clerk in a shop I entered the other day remarked to the

world at large: <quote> " I'm tired to death of cricket, cricket and nothing

but cricket.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X55:2> I'm longing to see a good film." </quote>
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X56:2> Of course, one might wonder when she had last seen that on J.B.C.,

anyway!
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X57:2> Jamaica is still being forced to make do with one television channel

whose programming, in both the selection and the quality of its reproduction,

is poor and shoddy. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X58:2> As an example of the latter, at least one of the video-players

the station uses jiggles the images of advertisements and programmes to the

point where colours blur together, and any print on screen is indecipherable,

while sound quality is completely distorted by static.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X59:2> May <?> - <?/> be, the advertisers don't mind.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X60:2> Certainly, many of them don't care enough to bother to have mounted

new advertisements in the last ten years.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X61:2> Though this, possibly, creates a world record in the age of

T.V.-ads., it surely produces very few sales for the product concerned. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X62:2> But, it seems, no-one is really bothered, anyway, as the present 'normal'

programming ( sans cricket) now caters to the lowest common denominator of

public taste.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X63:2> The Jamaican people, however they feel about the matter, must put up

with the distasteful videos and certain recent nasty advertisements which

constantly invade the privacy of our homes.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X64:2> Some time ago, when complained about, the growing practice of using

unmusical pop-videos as fillers was discontinued by J.B.C., but it is back

again!
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X65:2> As these are sneaked on and pushed into odd corners right before and

after mis-timed programmes which one actually might want to view, there is

little choice in the matter of whether or not one views such videos! </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X66:2> <h> Indictment </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X67:2> It is an indictment of us as a nation that we put up with a

government policy which allows us only one, government-controlled T.V.

channel, while <w> Barbados' </w> population, which is only a tenth of

Jamaica's population, enjoys the programmes of one local station - similar to

J.B.C. - and several others which are channelled in by satellite, re-

transmitted locally in scrambled form, and de-scrambled by boxes offered to

the general populace at reasonable rent.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X68:2> Our television policy simply has the usual philosophy:
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X69:2> Any rubbish is good enough for the masses! </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X70:2> We have gone from one decade to the next, from one election to

the next, with nothing but promises of another T.V. station.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X71:2> Isn't it extraordinary that the one additional television licence

that was eventually granted, was given to a company which has proved unable

to find the money to put its station on the air?
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X72:2> This, even though we all know that there are a number of local

persons with sufficient funds - and with the interest and access to expertise

- to run a second station.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X73:2> However, while the deteriorating values of the television,

constantly glorifying violence, are not regarded by either of our political

parties to be anything more than a 'froth-and-bubble' matter, even with the

spin-off of our rising crime rate, our one station always becomes a key

institution at election time.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X74:2> This is curious in view of the fact that the use to which it has so

far been put, the putting out of political ads and jingles, has been proved

to be completely ineffective. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X75:2> <h> Election violence </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-001#X76:2> For some decades now, Jamaica has witnessed an escalation in

violence at election time.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X77:2> This is generally admitted to have been encouraged by relying

heavily on public meetings and harangues.
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X78:2> Such confrontations of verbal violence cause bloody, physical

clashes between the party faithful, which both parties profess to abhor,

while trucking and bus-ing in numbers of their followers!
<ICE-JA:W2E-001#X79:2> Clearly, much of the physical violence, the political murders, can

be stopped by removing the emphasis on such public meetings; by scheduling,

in their place, a series of political debates on television, together with

interviews conducted by quick-witted, knowledgeable, unbiased and outspoken

media personalities. </I>
