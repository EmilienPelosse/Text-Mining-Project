<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#1:1> <h> IDT orders the reinstatement of Flour Mills worker </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#2:1> The Industrial Disputes Tribunal ( IDT), in a majority award, has

ordered the re- instatement of a former stacker at the Jamaica Flour Mills,

whose dismissal based on medical reports sparked a strike in March. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#3:1> The employee, Mark Poole, was fired on February 29, on the grounds

that he was unable to carry out his job due to medical reason.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#4:1> He had been employed by there since March, 1990, as a stacker. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#5:1> His dismissal sparked a strike which lasted over a week, as the

workers refused to abide by an order from the IDT to resume work after

efforts at the Ministry of Labour failed to resolve the issue. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#6:1> The company said that he had carried out his duties satisfactorily

until May, 1991, when they received a medical report from Dr. Errol Campbell,

indicating that he had a back problem and recommending that he be assigned

work which did not involve <quote> " the lifting of too much weight."

</quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#7:1> The company said that no alternative job could be found which

would involve lighter duties. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#8:1> The company contended that: Poole's own medical reports clearly

established that he was unfit for the job; they did not and still do not have

any position for which he is qualified, providing lighter work; and, the

termination of his service was in keeping with his own doctor's medical

reports. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#9:1> The NWU, which represented Poole, said that the company had

complied with the doctor's request by shifting him from stacker to general

worker, which involved much less lifting.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#10:1> Poole continued physiotherapy treatment until a doctor's certificate

was issued on November 15, 1991, indicating that the aches had disappeared.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#11:1> After the doctor's report, the company asked that he resigned or be

fired. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#12:1> The union said it disagreed with the company's decision on the

grounds that Poole had recovered sufficiently and the duties of the general

worker had helped him in recovering. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#13:1> They said that the company acted hastily in dismissing him and

that he should be reinstated, without infringement to his status as an

employee of the company. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#14:1> The Tribunal awarded that Poole be reinstated with full wages as

from March 1, 1992.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#15:1> Chairman of the panel, L.P. Brown, and <w> workers' </w>

representative, Irvin Jones, supported the award, but <w> employers' </w>

representative, Chester Burgess, did not. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#16:1> The JFM was represented by Robert Baugh, attorney-at-law, and the

union by Vincent Morrison, deputy island supervisor.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#17:1> Secretary to the panel was Miss D.A. Clarke. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#18:2> <h> Canadian journalist for Speakers Gallery </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#19:2> Canadian journalist, Mr. Oakland Ross, will be the next speaker

at Speakers Gallery - a monthly lecture series sponsored by the Bustamante

Institute of Public & International Affairs, a non-governmental organization.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#20:2> For a decade, from 1980 until 1990, Mr. Ross was a foreign

correspondent for Canada's leading newspaper, the Toronto Globe and Mail.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#21:2> From Latin America and the Caribbean, he has reported on

political turmoil in Jamaica and Haiti, communist rule in Cuba, civil war in

El Salvador, guerrilla campaigns in Nicaragua, anti-government protests in

Chile, and the day-to-day lives of ordinary people - from northern Mexico to

southern Argentina. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#22:2> In Africa, he has covered wars and strife in Mozambique, Angola,

Ethiopia, the Sudan, and South Africa, and has travelled the length and

monks in Tanzania, sacred crocodiles in the Ivory Coast, and mountain

herd-boys in Lesotho. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#23:2> Winner of three national newspaper award citations in Canada and

recipient of a special award from the Inter-America Press Association, Mr.

Ross has appeared frequently on television and radio in Canada to comment

about life and events in Africa and Latin America. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#24:2> Mr. Ross will be Guest Speaker at the Speakers Gallery today at

the PCJ Auditorium at 5:30 p.m.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#25:2> He will recount many of his stories and share some of the impressions

he has gathered during almost ten years of living in, and writing about, two

of the world's most troubled but also fascinating regions. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#72:5> By Lloyd Williams </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#73:5> KINGSTON, Jamaica, </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#74:5> August 12, NICA: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#75:5> A RECORD high 1,300 lb. of <quote> " high quality" </quote>

Narcotics Police.
<ICE-JA:W2C-019#76:5> They were acting on a tip from the United States Drug Enforcement

Administration office here. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#77:5> The drug is worth US$13.570 million approximately J$305.325

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#78:5> It was the largest amount of cocaine seized here by the police

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#79:5> Around 5 p.m., the local DEA office passed on a tip from its

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#83:5> During the three-hour operation which lasted from 6 p.m. to 9

p.m., the police boarded and searched several pleasure boats, including some

<ICE-JA:W2C-019#84:5> However, no cocaine was found. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-019#85:5> But the Narcotics Police were convinced of the accuracy of the
