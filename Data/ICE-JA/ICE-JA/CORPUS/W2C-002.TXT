<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#39:2> <h> Big fines coming for night noise </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#40:2> THE fine for persons convicted for playing loud music at nights

will soon move from the present

$40 to

$10,000 or three months imprisonment. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#41:2> With a second conviction for the same offence, the operator of

the offending sound system will be liable to a fine of

$15,000 or six months imprisonment. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#42:2> National Security and Justice Minister K.D. Knight gave those

assurances as he announced that legislation was in the making to deal with

night noises that disturb the peace. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#43:2> He was speaking on Tuesday at the launch of the JIS sponsored

series, " Face to Face Forum" held at the Meadowbrook High School.
<ICE-JA:W2C-002#44:2> Other Government Ministers present included Robert Pickersgill,

Easton Douglas, Carlyle Dunkley and Burchell Whiteman. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#45:2> Mr. Knight also said that owners of the properties on which the

music is transmitted, would also be liable, to <quote> " heavy" </quote> and

<quote> " realistic fines" </quote> . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#46:2> He, therefore, warned sound system operators and property owners

to <quote> " beware" <quote> as the legislation would soon be enacted.
<ICE-JA:W2C-002#47:2> He expressed the view that the impending legislation would <quote> "

have a deterrent effect and soon children will be able to study in their

neighbourhoods without being subject to this ( night noises) kind of

activity." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#48:2> Minister Knight pointed out that at present the law stipulates

that sound systems should not allow music to extend beyond 100 yards of the

source of the music.
<ICE-JA:W2C-002#49:2> He noted that existing law makes a convicted offender liable to pay

$40. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#50:2> The existing law also stipulates that the Police, on being

alerted to a case of noise pollution, are obliged: firstly to speak to the

operators, asking them to turn down the sound; secondly, if the volume is

turned up again after the police leave the equipment may be seized.
<ICE-JA:W2C-002#51:2> Thirdly, on seizing the equipment the police are required to get the

consent of the Clerk of the Court to prosecute the offender. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#52:2> The National Security Minister expressed the view that the

present system of administering justice to offending sound system operators

was cumbersome. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#77:4> Mr. Messado said yesterday that <quote> " Instead of the

</quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#78:4> The Press Association is planning to promote a series of national

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#80:4> CUP has written to the PAJ requesting that it convenes a series

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#82:4> Speaking yesterday at the Lions Club of New Kingston luncheon

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-002#83:4> Mr. Messado challenged the Lion's club members that they ( in
