<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#1:1> <h> <bold> Information Technology impact on business and maritime

communities </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#2:1> Paul Taylor, Contributor </h> </p> <O> photograph </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#3:1> <it> As we approach the close of the twentieth century, now is an

opportune time to examine how information technology ( IT) has impacted our

world.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#4:1> This is the first in a series that traces some of the major IT

developments over the last three years, in which Port Computer Services has

played a pivotal role. </it> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#5:1> <bold> UNDOUBTEDLY, THE </bold> world has entered an era of

tremendous change with concepts such as globalisation, right-sizing and

business processes re-engineering being today's clichés.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#6:1> Equally, talk of information technology and what is now termed the

information age have become common-place. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#7:1> To be sure, information technology ( IT), in the form of computers

and telecommunications, is creating an environment in which information moves

at rapid speed, and this can have positive spin-offs for the businesses

operating in a competitive environment.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#8:1> Indeed, the changing realities of the global marketplace have resulted

in a paradigm shift in the ways businesses function and have largely hastened

the adoption of appropriate technologies to ensure survival in the short,

medium and long term. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#9:1> In a real sense, the information revolution is fundamentally

changing the way we do business, communicate with clients, market products,

source supplies and find buyers.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#10:1> Additionally, new <quote> " virtual industries" </quote> ( industries

that exist electronically but do not have any physical premises) such as

virtual shopping malls and banks have sprung up in the past few years that

were not even dreamt of previously. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#11:1> In the Jamaican maritime sector, Port Computer Services Ltd. (

PCS), the Shipping Association of Jamaica's wholly owned computer subsidiary,

has being charged with the responsibility to spearhead IT developments on the

Port of Kingston and the maritime sector in general. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#12:1> PCS has led the way in the development of the locally shipping

industry's web site: <quote> " seaportsofjamaica.com" </quote> web site.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#13:1> The only interactive web site of its kind in the Caribbean basin,

this site showcases Jamaica's shipping industry to the world. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#14:1> Interest in the site, which features Jamaica's port facilities,

shipping companies and related agencies has continued to grow with positive

feedback being received from Internet surfers locally and abroad. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#15:1> Port Computer Services Ltd. in a pioneering role used the

intranet technology in conceiving, developing and implementing a one of a

kind shipping industry's Wide Area Network ( WAN).
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#16:1> In 1995 PCS embarked on this develo <space> pment to revolutionise

the process of information dissemination within the shipping industry, thus

making it more flexible and accessible to others who wish to share in the

transfer of information. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#17:1> <h> <bold> Objective </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#18:1> The objectives that drove the project have been concerned with

linking all existing local area networks ( LANs) within the shipping industry

to facilitate information sharing among members of the shipping fraternity.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#19:1> Some of the benefits that WAN provides to connect users include:

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#20:1> &dot; The ability to send and retrieve electronic mail and

associated documents to and from other users of the network, which has

contributed to the reduction in the number of faxed transmissions between

companies and the timely delivery of information to users. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#21:1> &dot; All companies connected to the WAN can access port

statistical information and other data in order to assist in the effective

management of organisational resources. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#22:1> &dot; Through the introduction of the WAN the door has been

opened for the utilisation of more Client/Server oriented software.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#23:1> This has resulted in less waiting time during the running of

interactive jobs, thus speeding-up the rate at which information is processed

and produced. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#24:1> &dot; The WAN has facilitated a more centralised and secured

database, while significant improvements in systems response time have also

been witnessed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#25:1> Given the competitive environment in which businesses must

operate, these and other benefits provided by the WAN can only augur well for

the internal and external customers of the shipping companies who utilise the

network. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#26:1> Indeed, the competitive nature of the business environment today

and the increased focus on customer care and satisfaction have played an

intrinsic role in the push to introduce and adopt emerging technologies to

improve the efficacy of businesses, as has been demonstrated by PCS and its

work in the local Shipping Industry. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#27:1> While computerisation has been mostly concerned with automating

existing business function, we are now moving away from thinking about

managing data towards managing information.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#28:1> By that it is meant, what information do we have about our customers

and can we use it to help us manage our organisations better while at the

same time effectively meeting the needs of our customers. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#29:1> The electronic manifest transfer initiative now underway in the

local Shipping Industry has been geared at just that, improving customer

service.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#30:1> This initiative will result in major improvements in efficiency in

the cargo clearance process on the Port of Kingston. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#31:1> The plan is to have shipping manifest information sent

electronically 24 hours before vessel arrival in the required format to

Jamaican Customs.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#32:1> Customs will then issue authorisation in the form of a certificate

within 24 hours of the vessel's arrival.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#33:1> The whole process will result in a significant reduction in the time

it takes to clear cargo, as data will now be received and processed even

while the ship is on the high seas. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#34:1> This means that a process which traditionally required numerous

copies of documents that was hand written at considerable clerical cost, with

goods taking several weeks to be cleared <-> ; </-> will be replaced by a

system that allows for clearance within a day.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#35:1> Additionally, the system will result in substantial savings to the

importer with less money spent on storage which will translate into savings

for customers. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#36:1> The electronic checking of customs entries will also

substantially reduce the high rate of errors and double entries in submitted

manifests.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#37:1> With goods being cleared in 24 hours there will be considerably

improved inventory management and less money tied up in goods.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#38:1> The process will also eliminate the arduous pre-clearance of

documents.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#39:1> ( to be continued) </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#40:2> <h> Impact on the business and maritime communities  <it>

Information Technology </it> </h> </p> <O> text </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#41:2> <bold> THE USE </bold> of technological systems to promote

internationally acceptable standards of productivity and efficiency has

caused ports in the developing world to have a level of equipment capability

that far exceeds other industries.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#42:2> This is particularly true of ports such as the Port of Kingston,

which handles a large volume of transhipment cargo. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#43:2> The Internet and other emerging technologies are also impacting

all functional areas of today's organisations, chiefly the marketing

function.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#44:2> Highly targeted marketing campaigns, for example, are becoming

commonplace. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#45:2> With information available more quickly, at much lower cost, and

with much less need for large and cumbersome administrative structures, the

whole concept of centralised and hierarchical management is now coming under

scrutiny. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#46:2> <h> <bold> Interests </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#47:2> More and more shipping interests are discovering that in the

future, the most successful companies will be made up of people who have

quick and easy access to the information that they require to make informed

choices about the best way to meet their <w> customers' </w> needs and,

crucially, will be given the responsibility and authority to act to guarantee

customer satisfaction in a timely manner. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#48:2> As Fred Luthans so ably articulated in his book, Organisational

Behaviour, " it is the knowledge worker, armed with the requisite skills to

master the demands of the organisation of the future, who stands the greatest

chance of succeeding and prospering in tomorrow's world". </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#49:2> According to Luthans, for organisations to grow and develop they

will have to embrace what he refers to as the philosophy of the Learning

Organisation, where the organisation's internal customers are continually

learning and are empowered to bring acquired skills and knowledge to improve

and enhance the productivity and profitability of the organisation. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#50:2> Here, information technology will define how authority and

responsibility are delegated and how persons in functional areas of the

organisation interact to enhance organisational value. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#51:2> If there is one vital word that provides a focus on what is being

said here, the word is <quote> 'relationship' </quote> .
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#52:2> For relationships within organisations will change-- between the

people at the centre and those closest to the customer in this information

age.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#53:2> Relationships across organisations will also change--partnerships and

alliances will become increasingly necessary to meet rapidly changing

circumstances, as evidenced in the moves by shipping lines in the global

shipping arena to form a number of alliances and consortia to benefit from

the attendant economies of scales provided.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#54:2> Most important of all, it should be noted that relationships between

organisations and their customers will change. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#55:2> <h> <bold> Revolution </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#56:2> The information revolution presents us with many challenges,

whether we are in shipping or some other industry, but it also provides us

with the tools and the means to meet them.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#57:2> Every individual should get him/herself prepared through training and

hands on experience in order to have a good chance of succeeding. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#58:2> To be sure, information technology is revolutionising, and will

continue to revolutionise, the way we work, and the next few years will see

major changes in the way organisations in the maritime industry and elsewhere

are structured and managed.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#59:2> These changes will be demanded by customers, made possible by new

technology, and will be embraced by those organisations that want to survive,

and moreover, succeed. </p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#60:3> <h> <bold> The challenges of communication technology </bold>

</h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#61:3> <bold> THERE IS </bold> hardly an office today that is not

computerised.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#62:3> The change has been swift and adaptation equally quick.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#63:3> The use of fax machines, and the technology, which enables

teleconferences and videoconferences, has rapidly increased the speed of

messages and reduced the need to travel to meet face to face. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#64:3> Word-processing, graphics and desktop publishing have helped in

the quick production of polished presentations and enable quick revisions as

well as the easy personalisation of business letters and other documents.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#65:3> It is difficult to remember what we used to do before the

computer. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#66:3> Yet, when IBM introduced its first personal computer in 1981, it

hoped to sell only 250,000 machines over the life of the product.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#67:3> Ten years later, they had sold more than 55 million personal

computers in the United States alone. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#68:3> However, there are those who believe that computerisation in all

its forms has been embraced in too wholesale a fashion by some businesses at

the expense of the personal touch. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#69:3> Many would-be customers are turned away from businesses,

intimidated by the numerous options of voicemail messages that they use.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#70:3> It is now a matter of debate, whether businesses that offer a

service, like banks, or airlines, should embrace this technological

application in such a wholesale manner.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#71:3> In fact, there are an increasing number of businesses in the United

States that are going back to the old way of doing business, with the

emphasis being on the personal touch. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#72:3> The promised " paperless environment" of the new computerised

age, has also not been realised.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#73:3> In fact, there are those who can show evidence that their dependency

on the filing cabinet has dramatically increased, since the computer. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#74:3> Yet, as we adapt to the various nuances of this new technological

age, there are still many challenges to be faced, and still much to be

learned about the hundreds of applications of computer technology.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#75:3> Perhaps no industry is more affected than the communication industry.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#76:3> Many are still wrestling with the mysteries and potential of

communicating on-line, as well as interfacing computer to computer with the

print and electronic media - still the principal channels of public

communication. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#77:3> It is against this background that the local arm of the

International Association of Business Communicators ( IABC) will be mounting

a one-day Seminar on Friday November 5, at the Jamaica Pegasus Hotel.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#78:3> Principal speaker, Charles Pizzo, who is IABC Vice Chairman and

Principal of a New Orleans- based Public Relations Agency, PR Inc, states

that Information Technology for Communicators, ( one of the topics he will be

presenting), is a practical response to concerns surrounding the issues of

emerging technology and the need for these to be presented in a practical and

concise way.
<ICE-JA:W2B-038#79:3> His presentation on <quote> " Strategies for Online Communicators"

</quote> , is also aimed particularly at the communicator who is expected to

provide information to management instantly. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-038#80:3> <quote> " We will" </quote> , he explains, <quote> " examine

strategic solutions that augment existing communications programmes with best

practices for the online era including research, media relations, crisis

communication, investor relations and marketing communications." </quote>

</p> </I>
