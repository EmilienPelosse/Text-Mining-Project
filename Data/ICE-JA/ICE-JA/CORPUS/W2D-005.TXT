<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#101:2> <h> <bold> APPENDIX I </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#102:2> <h> <bold> <ul> HINTS ON ESSAY WRITING IN LITERATURE </ul>

</bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#103:2> You will be expected to write an essay of your prescribed texts.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#104:2> In these essays you will be asked to examine aspects of the texts

that have been either raised in lectures or discussed in tutorials.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#105:2> The main purpose of the exercise of essay writing is to help you to

develop a critical and analytical sense.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#106:2> This exercise also helps to discipline <}> <-> an </-> <+> and </+>

</}> give shape to intellectual responses provoked by your reading and

discussion of literary texts. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#107:2> <bold> CONSEQUENTLY, IN NO ESSAY WILL IT BE NECESSARY TO RECOUNT

THE PLOT OF A PARTICULAR WORK OR REPEAT BIBLIOGRAPHICAL DETAILS ABOUT THE

AUTHOR CONCERNED. </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#108:2> You can safely assume that these are already known to your tutor

and that what is being tested is your ability to organize ideas. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#109:2> <h> <bold> RECOMMENDED PROCEDURE: </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#110:2> 1. Study your title carefully and the rubric which follows,

e.g., <quote> " Discuss" </quote> or <quote> " examine" </quote> or <quote> "

illustrate" </quote> etc.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#111:2> Make sure you know what the question means and its full implications

for the text to which it refers.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#112:2> Essay topics fall into two general categories: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#113:2> a. The value judgement which needs to be discussed and defined,

e.g., <quote> " The novel is too concerned with political ideas to be

successful as a novel." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#114:2> b. The question which requires you to examine some aspect of the

text, e.g., <quote> " Examine how the method of narration contributes to the

novel's success." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#115:2> 2. Make a rough plan, noting all ideas, first reactions that

appear relevant to the topic.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#116:2> These may consist of personal impressions, observations by critics,

etc.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#117:2> Critical works are useful in providing a fuller response to a text.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#118:2> Remember, however, that in any essay there is room for a diversity

of opinion and you should only accept a critic's opinions if they convince

you.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#119:2> The critic is essentially a guide.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#120:2> Your priority must be to define <bold> your own </bold> response to

the text and to convince! </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#121:2> 3. Make a definite and detailed plan.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#122:2> At this point you should have a clear idea of what is not relevant

to your topic and also be sure of your response to the chosen topic.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#123:2> Your introduction and <}> <-> cnclusion </-> <+> conclusion </+>

</}> could now be written out.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#124:2> The former should be as direct as possible, setting out what you see

as the main issues raised by the topic.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#125:2> <bold> No essay on Zobel should begin <quote> " Zobel was born in

Martinique in the year&" </quote>
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#126:2> Your conclusion should sum up and restate the main thrust of your

arguments.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#127:2> Do not introduce any new ideas in your conclusion.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#128:2> The ordering and linking of paragraphs in the body of the essay is

indicative of the development of your thoughts.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#129:2> Also, each paragraph must have its own subject which relates to an

aspect of the essay topic being treated. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-005#130:2> 4. With you plan made, you are able to concentrate on style and

expression when writing your essay.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#131:2> Avoid long, verbose sentences.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#132:2> Stick to a clear, formal English style.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#133:2> Remove all eccentricities from your style, whether they take the

form of inflated, flowery English or an overly subjective approach using

exclamations etc.
<ICE-JA:W2D-005#134:2> Your ultimate aim <-> is </-> in this regard is to achieve a style

of your own which can clearly and persuasively articulate your ideas. </p>
