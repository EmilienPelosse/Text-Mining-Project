<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#1:1> <h> <bold> Lead & Health </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#2:1> <h> <bold> How can lead affect me? </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#3:1> If you are in an area where the soil is contaminated, lead can get

into your body in two ways. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#4:1> You can swallow it in the form of dust that is on your fingers,

fruits or anything else that goes into your mouth.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#5:1> You can also inhale it when dust is present in the air you breathe.

</p> <O> picture </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#6:1> When the dust you swallow gets into your intestines, some of the

lead may pass into your bloodstream.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#7:1> Unfortunately, all of the lead that is breathed into your lungs will

be absorbed by your bloodstream. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#8:1> Most of the lead in your blood is then passed into your bones and

teeth.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#9:1> These act as storage areas for lead.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#10:1> Bones and teeth will continue to collect lead as long as it is in

your blood. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#11:1> Many people with lead <quote> 'in storage' </quote> appear

healthy for years. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#12:1> <h> <bold> So what is the problem? </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#13:1> When the body is put under stress, the lead <quote> 'in storage

</quote> can go back into your bloodstream.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#14:1> Things that put stress on your body include pregnancy and

breastfeeding, or sickness. </p> <O> picture </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#15:1> This does not mean that women should avoid breastfeeding.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#16:1> A mother's milk is very important for the proper development of a

newborn. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#17:1> Lead causes many symptoms including extreme fatigue, high blood

pressure, kidney problems and nervous disorders. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#18:1> In children, high blood lead levels may lead to hearing loss and

learning disabilities.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#19:1> Other symptoms can be mistaken for the flu. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#20:1> Lead is most dangerous to children under six years of age because

their bodies are still developing.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#21:1> These children may absorb up to four times more lead than an adult

who is exposed to the same amount. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#22:1> Pregnant women and nursing mothers are also known to absorb more

lead from their environment.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#23:1> This can be passed on to their children. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#24:1> <h> <bold> Things to do </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#25:1> A doctor can get a blood test done for lead.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#26:1> Pregnant women and children have the highest risk. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#27:1> Some very simple steps can limit your intake of lead and help

keep your family lead-safe. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#28:1> <bold> 1. </bold> Wash your face and hands regularly and

carefully clean under fingernails.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#29:1> Dirty fingers may transfer dust containing lead onto food or any

other objects intended for your mouth. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#30:1> <bold> 2. </bold> Eat foods with a lot of iron and calcium as

these minerals may actually lessen the amount of lead being absorbed into

your bloodstream. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#31:1> <bold> 3. </bold> Ensure that there is adequate Vitamin C in your

diet by having oranges and other citrus fruits as well as green, leafy

vegetables.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#32:1> Vitamin C helps your body absorb iron from the other foods you eat.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#33:1> <bold> 4. </bold> Avoid foods with a lot of fat.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#34:1> Too much fat in your diet makes it easier for lead to enter your

bloodstream. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#35:1> <bold> 5. </bold> Wash all food that you eat in a <quote> 'raw'

</quote> state.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#36:1> Fruits and vegetables may have small particles of dust on them.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#37:1> If they are not peeled or washed properly, you may swallow any lead

present in the dust. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#38:1> <bold> 6. </bold> Rinse any toys that young children may put into

their mouths.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#39:1> Children may ingest lead when they suck objects that have been picked

up from the floor. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#40:1> <bold> 7. </bold> Spread a towel on the floor or in the yard

before you put down a baby. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#41:1> <bold> 8. </bold> Clean houses with wet mops and damp rags

instead of brooms and feather-dusters.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#42:1> Wipe all horizontal surfaces like window sills, kitchen counters, and

tabletops at least once per day. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#43:1> <bold> 9. </bold> Wipe your feet on a damp mat or leave shoes

worn outside at the doorway before entering a home. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#44:1> <bold> 10. </bold> Keep pets outside the house and never allow

them to sit on chairs or beds inside. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#45:1> <bold> 11. </bold> Alcohol and tobacco are known to encourage

lead absorption.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#46:1> Keep your use of these drugs at a minimum or avoid them altogether.

</p> <O> picture </O> <O> caption </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#47:1> <bold> 12. </bold> Plant grass or other foliage if there are

large patches of bare dirt in your yard.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#48:1> Grass <quote> 'covers' </quote> dirt and prevents it from being

stirred up by breeze. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#49:1> <bold> 13. </bold> Insist that shoes be worn outside.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#50:1> It is very important for children in particular to keep their feet

covered while outdoors.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#51:1> This will make it less likely for them to carry dust back into the

house. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#52:1> <h> <bold> Remember </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#53:1> * Lead enters the body through the mouth and nose </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#54:1> * Some of the lead that gets into the blood will be stored in

bones and teeth </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#55:1> * People with a lot of lead stored in their bodies may appear

healthy and normal for years </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#56:1> * When the body is stressed, lead comes out of <quote> 'storage'

</quote> and goes back into blood </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#57:1> * High blood lead levels can cause illness and learning

deficiencies </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#58:1> * Symptoms of high blood lead levels are often mistaken for the

flu </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#59:1> * To make certain, everybody exposed to lead contaminated soil

should get a blood test </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#60:1> * Calcium- and iron-rich food can reduce the uptake of lead </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#61:1> * Practising proper hygiene can keep lead from entering your body

</p> </I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#62:2> <h> <bold> Lead-safe Homes </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#63:2> <h> <bold> Why should I be concerned? </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#64:2> Lead can be absorbed into our bodies when we are exposed to it.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#65:2> Lead is bad for our health. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#66:2> Children less than five years old are the ones most at risk.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#67:2> High levels of lead in their blood may cause learning, behavioural,

or psychological problems. </p> <O> picture </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#68:2> Adults with high blood lead levels may suffer from <quote> " body

run- down" </quote> , high blood pressure, kidney and other problems. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#69:2> Soil in the area surrounding lead mines, smelters and battery

shops is usually contaminated with lead.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#70:2> Those of use who live in such areas have a higher risk of lead

exposure than persons living elsewhere in Jamaica. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#71:2> We must therefore take greater precautions to keep our houses and

property clean.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#72:2> Lead attaches itself to dust, so a home that is free of dust will be

safer. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#73:2> Lead may also be present in older paint.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#74:2> Therefore, paint that is peeling or cracked may be an added source of

lead exposure. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#75:2> <h> <bold> Tips for keeping homes lead-safe &dotted-line; </bold>

</h> </p> <O> picture </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#76:2> <bold> 1. </bold> Use wet mops to clean floors and damp rags to

clean furniture.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#77:2> They will pick up the dust you want to get rid of.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#78:2> Brooms and feather dusters will only spread dust which you can inhale

around the house. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#79:2> <bold> 2. </bold> Rinse the wet mops and rags you use for

cleaning and change the rinse water regularly.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#80:2> If you keep wetting them in the same water, then after a while you

will be spreading the dirt wherever you wipe. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#81:2> <bold> 3. </bold> Keep damp mats at all entrances to the house

and insist that boots worn outside be taken off at the door.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#82:2> Dry mats can remove dirt but not dust. </p> <O> picture </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#83:2> <bold> 4. </bold> Wash or vacuum rugs and carpets often.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#84:2> Sweeping them off won't clean away all the dust. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#85:2> <bold> 5. </bold> Put a cloth over your nose while cleaning so

that you won't inhale the dust. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#86:2> <bold> 6. </bold> Sprinkle water on any bare soil around your

house and encourage grass to grow.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#87:2> Wind picks up bare dirt and spreads it inside your house or on

clothes that have been hung up to dry.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#88:2> The habit of sweeping yards with a broom ( instead of a rake)

prevents grass from growing to create a lawn.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#89:2> Remember that a lot of grass in your yard will help to <quote> " keep

down" </quote> the dust. </p> <O> picture </O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#90:2> <bold> 7. </bold> Keep disinfectant and detergent on hand at all

times.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#91:2> These may be added to any water being used in rinsing or wiping. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#92:2> <h> <bold> What about the food in my house? </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#93:2> If they are not washed properly, fruits and raw vegetables may

carry lead on them. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#94:2> Remember that lead can be in the dirt.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#95:2> When fruits drop off trees and the dust is not properly scrubbed off,

children who eat the fruits may swallow lead. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#96:2> If raw vegetables and fruits are kept in open containers inside

the house, dust may collect on them.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#97:2> Use plastic bags or bowls that you can cover to store your produce.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#98:2> Peel ground provisions like potatoes, carrots and beet root

before cooking them.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#99:2> Rinse all the dust off food like mangoes and tomatoes just before

eating them. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#100:2> Wipe kitchen counters, cutting boards and dining tables with a

clean, damp cloth before using them. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#101:2> Drape a towel over dish-drainers so that dust which may contain

lead does not collect in the cups and plates left to dry. </p> <O> picture

<O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#102:2> <bold> Remember </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#103:2> * Lead enters the body when we breathe or ingest contaminated

soil, dust, air, food or water. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#104:2> * Paint may contain lead so it is harmful to swallow paint chips

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#105:2> * Lead in dust can be avoided by keeping your home clean and

keeping the community <quote> " green" </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#106:2> * Lead comes into houses with dust or dirty clothes and pets

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#107:2> * Contaminated dust can settle on furniture, floors, window

sills and countertops. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#108:2> * Breathe through a clean, damp cloth while dusting furniture or

sweeping the yard and remember that damp rags and wet mops clean dust better

than feather dusters and brooms </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#109:2> * Wash all doormats, rugs, carpets and cushion cases regularly

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#110:2> * Wash all fruits and vegetables and store them in clean plastic

bags or covered containers </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#111:2> * Run tap water for two minutes before drinking or cooking </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#112:3> <h> <bold> Lead-safe children </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#113:3> <h> <bold> Is my child at risk? </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#114:3> The amount of lead in the soil of some areas is much higher than

normal.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#115:3> Lead can get inside our bodies when we breathe dust or swallow

contaminated dirt.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#116:3> If too much lead enters the bloodstream, it can make us sick. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#117:3> Children tend to play in dirt and they are likely to put dirty

fingers, toys and fruits into their mouths.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#118:3> Children may even eat dirt.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#119:3> As a result, a child will take in more lead than a responsible

adult. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#120:3> Children and even foetuses are growing at a rapid rate.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#121:3> Their bodies may absorb up to four times the amount of lead that a

healthy adult in the same environment would. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#122:3> Because their bodies are developing, it is easier for lead to

accumulate inside them.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#123:3> Also, it is more likely that lead will harm them. </p> <O> picture

</O>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#124:3> A simple blood test can detect high levels of lead.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#125:3> It is very important for children who live in an area with elevated

lead levels to have their blood tested at least once per year. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#126:3> <h> <bold> How bad is it? </bold> / h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#127:3> Lead can only harm a child if it gets into the body.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#128:3> As long as lead remains in the soil it cannot affect health. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#129:3> Some children already have lead in their blood because of bad

habits. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#130:3> Children should not be allowed to play in yards where the soil

is high in lead.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#131:3> It is easy to inhale lead when dust is blowing around.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#132:3> They can swallow it when they eat dirt, suck their fingers or put

dirty toys and soothers in their mouths. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#133:3> Children can also get lead from mangoes and other fruits which

have not been properly washed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#134:3> It is also dangerous for them to touch dirty pets or other

animals which live outdoors.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#135:3> Dogs and goats may have a lot of dust in their fur.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#136:3> Even chickens and pigeons are likely to store dust in their

feathers. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#137:3> Also, those who go outside barefoot can bring dust with lead

back to their homes.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#138:3> Dirty feet can contaminate couches and beds which hold dust inside

the house. </p> <O> picture </O> <p 
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#139:3> <h> <bold> How do I keep my children <quote> " lead-safe" </quote> ?

</bold> </h> </p> <p 
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#140:3> You keep children lead-safe by preventing dirt and dust from

entering their bodies.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#141:3> It is that simple. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#142:3> Carefully wash their hands and faces after they have been

outside and whenever they are going to eat or sleep. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#143:3> Keep their fingernails short and very clean.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#144:3> You can also boil soothers and any toys which children like to suck.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#145:3> Teach them not to touch dogs, cats, or chickens that may be in

the yard.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#146:3> If they must play with pets, they should wash their hands and faces

when they are finished. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#147:3> It is also a good idea to bathe your pets regularly. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#148:3> Don't allow cats ( or any other animals) to sit on your child's

bed or on the sofa.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#149:3> As a general rule, animals should be kept outside the house. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#150:3> Allow children to play in grassy areas or on concrete surfaces

that have been washed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#151:3> Make them wear shoes or slippers when outside the house and take

them off before coming inside. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#152:3> Do not give them fatty foods too often, but increase their

intake of iron, calcium and vitamin C. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-016#153:3> Before the dangers of lead were well known, there were no

regulations to limit the concentration of lead in paint.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#154:3> Never allow a child to suck on furniture, window sills or walls.
<ICE-JA:W2D-016#155:3> Old paint on these surfaces may contain lead. </p> </I>
