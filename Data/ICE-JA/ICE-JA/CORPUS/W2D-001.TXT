<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#1:1> <h> <bold> Jamaican Standard Specification for </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#2:1> Single cold-reduced tinplate and single cold-reduced blackplate

</h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#3:1> 1. Scope </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#4:1> This standard applies to single cold-reduced electrolytic and

hot-dipped tinplate sheet in low carbon cold-reduced mild steel in nominal

thicknesses from 0.15 mm up to and including 0.49 mm and to single

cold-reduced blackplate sheet in low carbon mild steel in nominal thick <l>

nesses from 0.15 mm up to and including 0.49 mm. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#5:1> This standard does not apply to tinplate or blackplate in coils,

or to double-reduced tinplate or blackplate, or to material described

commercially as tinned sheets, steel sheets or electrolytic chromium/chromium

oxide coated steel ( TFS). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#6:1> <h> <bold> 2. Definitions </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#7:1> For the purpose of this standard the following definitions apply:

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#8:1> <bold> 2.1 blackplate. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#9:1> Cold-reduced low carbon mild steel sheet, not tinned, and normally not

oiled or otherwise treated. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#10:1> <bold> 2.2 consignment. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#11:1> A quantity of tinplate or blackplate of the same dimensions and

quality made available for despatch at the same time. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#12:1> <bold> 2.3 differentially coated tinplate. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#13:1> Electrolytic tinplate, one surface of which carries a heavier tin

coating than the other. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#14:1> <bold> 2.4 finish </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#15:1> <bold> 2.4.1 </bold> <it> bright finish. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#16:1> A surface provided by a flow-brightened tin coating on a smooth

finish steel base. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#17:1> <bold> 2.4.2 </bold> <it> matt finish. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#18:1> A surface provided by an unmelted coating generally on a shot blast

finish steel base ( see <bold> 3.5 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#19:1> <bold> 2.4.3 </bold> silver finish.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#20:1> A surface provided by a flow- brightened tin coating on a highly

roughened shot blast finish steel base ( see <bold> 3.5 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#21:1> <bold> 2.4.4 </bold> <it> stone finish. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#22:1> A surface provided by a flow-brightened tin coating on a steel base

finish characterized by a directional pattern. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#23:1> <bold> 2.5 tinplate. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#24:1> Cold-reduced low carbon mild steel sheet coated on both surfaces with

tin, applied either by dipping in molten tin or by electro-deposition.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#25:1> Tinplate produced by the hot-dipping process is called hot-dipped

tinplate; that produced by electro-deposition is called electrolytic

tinplate. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#26:1> <h> <bold> 3. Conditions of manufacture and utilization </bold>

</h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#27:1> <bold> 3.1 </bold> The methods of manufacture of tinplate and

blackplate are the province of the producer. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#28:1> <bold> 3.2 </bold> The methods of using tinplate and blackplate

are the province of the user. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#29:1> <bold> 3.3 </bold> The tin used for the coating of tinplate shall

have a purity of not less than 99.75% </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#30:1> <bold> 3.4 </bold> The chemical composition of the steel shall be

agreed upon between the producer and the purchaser provided that it is

consistent with the requirements of this standard ( see clause <bold> 14

</bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#31:1> <bold> 3.5 </bold> At the time they are made available by the

producer and under normal conditions of transport and storage, tinplate and

blackplate are suitable for surface treatments such as established lacquering

and printing operations.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#32:1> Appropriate grades and tempers are also suit <l> able for shaping

operations such as stamping, drawing, folding and bending, and assembly work

such as joint forming, soldering ( tinplate only) and welding.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#33:1> The purchaser's order requirements shall be consistent with the end

use of the product. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#34:1> <h> <bold> 3.6 Surface finishes </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#35:1> <bold> 3.6.1 </bold> There are four recognized commercial

finishes for electrolytic tinplate namely, bright, stone, matt and silver (

see <bold> 2.4 </bold> ) .
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#36:1> The appearance is governed by: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#37:1> ( a) the surface characteristics of the steel base which

principally result from control <l> led preparation of the work rolls used

during the final stages of temper rolling; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#38:1> ( b) the mass of the coating applied; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#39:1> ( c) whether the tin layer is melted or unmelted. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#40:1> <bold> 3.6.2 </bold> Blackplate shall be available in the same

steel base surface finishes. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#41:1> <h> <bold> 3.7 Surface treatments ( electrolytic tinplate only)

</bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#42:1> <bold> 3.7.1 </bold> <it> Passivation treatment. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#43:1> A chemical or electrochemical treatment applied to the surface of

electrolytic tinplate to produce a surface of improved resistance to

discolouration and superior lacquering and printing quality.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#44:1> The usual procedure is cathodic treatment in a solution of sodium

dichromate ( see <bold> 14.2.4 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#45:1> <bold> 3.7.2 </bold> <it> Oiling. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#46:1> Normally the surface of electrolytic tinplate has applied to it a

very thin coating of an oil which is suitable for food packaging ( see <bold>

14.2.1 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#47:1> <bold> NOTE 1. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#48:1> When ordering tinplate and blackplate it is recommended that the

purpose of manufacture for which the tinplate or blackplate is intended

should be stated.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#49:1> The purchaser should indicate to the producer the direction of

rolling required. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#50:1> <bold> NOTE 2. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#51:1> It is recommended that, if required, the producer supplies to the

purchaser such details of the steel <l> making process as may assist the

purchaser in his efficient use of the material.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#52:1> It is further recommended that the purchaser be informed of any

alterations in the method of manufacture which will significantly affect the

properties of the purchased product.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#53:1> Similarly, it is recommended that the purchaser informs the producer

of modifications in the fabrication methods which will significantly affect

the way in which the purchased product is used. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#54:1> <h> <bold> 4. Material grading </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#55:1> <bold> Electrolytic tinplate, standard grade. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#56:1> Standard grade electrolytic tinplate represents the normal production

of lines employing the usual inspection and classification procedures.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#57:1> In normal conditions of storage and use, standard grade electrolytic

tinplate permits lacquering and printing over the entire surface. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#58:1> <bold> NOTE. </bold> Electrolytic tinplate, second grade, is

available in certain countries.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#59:1> It represents the best sheets reject <l> ed from the standard grade

but may contain sheets exhibiting surface imperfections, tinning, shape and

other defects to a minor extent ( but not pinholes or off-gauge material).
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#60:1> Suitability for lacquering and printing over the entire surface is

not guaranteed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#61:1> <bold> 4.2 Hot-dipped tinplate. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#62:1> After coating, hot-dipped tinplate is inspected and graded as

follows: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#63:1> <bold> 4.2.1 First grade or primes. </bold> Tinplate which at the

time of despatch is free from defects readily visible to the unaided eye.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#64:1> In normal conditions of storage and use it is suitable for lacquering

or printing over the entire surface of the sheet. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#65:1> <bold> 4.2.2 Second grade or seconds. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#66:1> Tinplate which at the time of dispatch has visible imperfec <l> tions

of moderate magnitude or frequency.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#67:1> Lacquering or printing over the entire surface of the sheet is not

guaranteed. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#68:1> NOTE. The term <mention> 'standard grade' </mention> is applied

to hot-dipped tinplate which is inspected during processing and from which

material not of first or second grade is rejected.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#69:1> The accepted material however, is not segregated into first and

second grades. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#70:1> <bold> 4.3 Blackplate. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#71:1> Blackplate is line inspected during processing.

<ICE-JA:W2D-001#73:1> Blackplate is liable to rust, but at the time it is made available by

the producer the material is suitable for normal lacquer <l> ing and printing

over the entire surface. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#74:1> <bold> NOTE. Second grade. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#75:1> In certain countries, blackplate rejected during line inspection <}>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#93:1> <bold> 5.6 Marking of differentially coated tinplate. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#94:1> In order to distinguish material having differen <l> tial coatings,

<ICE-JA:W2D-001#95:1> In all cases, both the surface to be marked and the surface which is

to be piled uppermost shall be clearly designated on the contract by the

<ICE-JA:W2D-001#96:1> Generally the marking shall be made on the heavily coated surface in

alternate lines should be interrupted, or geometrical patterns may be used.

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#98:1> <h> <bold> 6. Temper classifications </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#99:1> <bold> 6.1 </bold> The term <mention> 'temper' </mention> , when

various factors which contribute to the fabrication characteristics of the

material.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#100:1> However, the Rockwell 30T hardness test ( HR 30T) is the best single

test available and serves as a guide to the properties of the material.

<ICE-JA:W2D-001#102:1> Hardness values at which the producer shall aim are specified in

required by reference to the appropriate temper classification as given in

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#104:1> <bold> 6.3 </bold> The hardness of tinplate and blackplate shall

be determined on samples selected in accordance with the requirements of

clause <bold> 8 </bold> and tested in accordance with requirements of clause

<bold> 10 </bold> .
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#105:1> When evaluating the hardness of tinplate and blackplate, average

<ICE-JA:W2D-001#106:1> The Rockwell hardness values given in table 4 are for tests

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#107:1> <bold> NOTE 1. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#108:1> The Rockwell hardness test is sensitive to the <quote> 'anvil'

</quote> effect and hence is affected by the thickness of the test specimen.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#109:1> The values given in table 4 are typical for nominal thicknesses in

the range of 0.25 mm to 0.30 mm.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#110:1> Material of the same metallurgical quality in 0.22 mm thickness, for

instance, would be one HR 30T unit higher and material of 0.43 mm thickness

one unit lower.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#111:1> Thinner material, for example 0.17 mm, may be 2 units higher.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#112:1> For a given temper classification, the corresponding HR 30T values

for blackplate may be as much as 4 units lower, depending on the age of the

material and the conditions of storage. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#113:1> <bold> NOTE 2. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#114:1> On relatively thin material the HR 15T test may be employed and the

values converted to HR 30T ( see appendix B). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#115:1> <bold> 6.4 </bold> The mechanical properties of continuously

annealed tinplate and blackplate and batch annealed material of the same HR

30T hardness are not identical.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#116:1> By agreement, the type of annealing, [ batch ( BA), or continuous (

CA)] may be specified when ordering for example, T 61 ( BA) or T 61 ( CA).

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#117:1> <bold> NOTE. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#118:1> HR 30T test values may be affected by the surface roughness of the

steel base obtained by the use of shot blast rolls in the final pass of

temper rolling. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#119:1> <h> <bold> 7. Dimensional requirements </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#120:1> <bold> 7.1 Thickness. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#121:1> Tinplate and blackplate are normally available in any nominal

thickness which is a multiple of 0.01 mm from 0.15 mm up to and including

0.49 mm.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#122:1> The producer shall aim to produce the thickness ordered. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#123:1> <bold> 7.1.1 </bold> <it> Determination of thickness. </it>
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#124:1> The average thickness of the consignment shall be deter <l> mined by

weighing whole sheets, or by direct measurement using a micrometer. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#125:1> When the weighing technique is used, the mass of each whole

sheet is determined, its area is measured, and the thickness determined by

applying the formula as given in <bold> 7.1.1.1. </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#126:1> The mass of the sheet shall be determined to a precision of 2 g

and the dimensions of the sheet shall be measured to a precision of 0.5 mm.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#127:1> The thickness shall be stated to the nearest 0.001 mm. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-001#128:1> If the average thickness of a consignment is to be determined by

direct measurement, a hand-operated micrometer shall be used.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#129:1> It shall be accurate to 0.001 mm.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#130:1> The measurement of thickness shall be carried out at least 10 mm

from the trimmed edge.
<ICE-JA:W2D-001#131:1> In any case of dispute, retesting of thickness by the weighing

method only shall be employed. </p>

<ICE-JA:W2D-001#133:1> It is recommended that when a micrometer is used it should have a

ball ended shank anvil of approxi <l> mately 3 mm diameter, a curved surface

anvil of approximately 25 mm radius and a face diameter of ap <l> proximately

13 mm. </p>
