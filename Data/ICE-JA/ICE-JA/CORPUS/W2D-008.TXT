<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#3:1> <h> QUALIFICATIONS FOR ADMISSION </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#4:1> 1. Before registration, and before beginning the courses leading

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#5:1> ( a) the University Matriculation requirements for entry to a

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#6:1> ( b) the entry requirements of the Faculty of Arts and General

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#43:1> <h> <bold> CATEGORIES OF COURSES/WEIGHTING </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#44:1> 3. ( a) Each course is classified either as a <bold> University

course </bold> or as a <bold> Faculty course. </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#45:1> ( b) Each course is designated as appropriate to Level I, II or

III. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#46:1> ( c) <w> Beginners' </w> courses are sometimes offered in foreign

languages ( See Para. 4 below) </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#47:1> ( d) Courses have a normal weighting of three ( 3) credits,

except the Level III University Course ( UC3XO), and a few Faculty Courses,

whose weighting varies between 4 and 6. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#48:1> <h> <bold> <w> BEGINNERS' </w> COURSES IN LANGUAGES </bold> </h>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#49:1> 4. ( a) <w> Beginners' </w> courses may be offered in French,

Spanish, and any other foreign language that may be added from time to time.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#50:1> ( b) A candidate is <bold> not </bold> permitted to offer more

required for the degree. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#51:1> ( c) Qualifications - <w> Beginners' </w> courses are open to the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#52:1> ( i) those who have no previous knowledge of the language

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#53:1> ( ii) those who have a limited knowledge of the language

concerned, but no academic qualifications; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#54:1> ( iii) those with academic qualifications, but whose knowledge of

the language is deemed by the Department concerned to be inadequate for

admission to normal Level I courses. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#55:1> ( d) A student who has successfully completed a Semester 2 <w>

discretion of the Head of the Department con <l> cerned.

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#57:1> ( e) A <w> Beginners' </w> course cannot be offered as part of

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#61:1> <h> <bold> REQUIREMENTS FOR THE AWARD OF THE DEGREE </bold> </h>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#62:1> 6. In order to qualify for a degree a student must satisfy the

following requirements: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#63:1> I. Have completed a <bold> MINIMUM </bold> of 90 credits of which

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#64:1> ( a) 18 credits shall be for University Courses ( 6 credits at

each of the three levels) </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#65:1> ( b) at least 72 credits in Faculty Courses shall be for Level I,

II and III courses of which </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#66:1> ( i) a maximum of 30 credits shall be for Level I courses (

including University courses) of which, ( except in the case of BA Theology)

at least 6 shall be from within the Arts group of subjects outside of the

declared Major/Spe <l> cial. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#67:1> ( ii) a minimum of 18 credits shall be for Level II courses ( not

including University courses). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#68:1> ( iii) a minimum of 9 credits shall be for courses exclusively

Level III ( not including University Courses) <+> . </+> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#69:1> II. ( a) on entry declare a Major, or a Special, or double Major,

( see 7f below), and </p>

<p> ( b) satisfy the requirements for the declared Major(s). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#70:1> 7. ( a) A Major or Special must be declared from among the follow

<l> ing subjects only: </p>

<p> English, French, Geography, History, History/Archaeology, Library

Studies, Linguistics, Mass Communications, Mathe <l> matics, Computer

Science, Spanish. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#71:1> ( b) A Major is made up of a minimum of 36 credits in a single

subject over the three levels.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#72:1> In Library Studies and Mass Communications, the Major requires a

minimum of 45 credits. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#73:1> ( c) A Special is made up of a minimum of 54 credits in the

subject over the three levels.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#74:1> Permission to declare a Special in any given subject must be sought

from the Head of the relevant Department. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#75:1> ( d) Component courses in a Major or Special are determined by

the Faculty Board on the recommendation of the relevant Department(s). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#76:1> ( e) In 1993-94 only the Departments of Geography and History

will offer Specials.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#77:1> Double Majors are not offered at present. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#78:1> ( f) Throughout the entire degree programme, <bold> no more than

30 credits </bold> may be selected from courses other than Arts courses,

except with the special permission of the Dean. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#79:1> <h> <bold> Guide to Registration </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#80:1> Full-time Level I students should register according to the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#81:1> 1. University course 6 credits </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#82:1> 2. Compulsory courses for Majors at least 6 credits </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#83:1> 3. Non-Major Arts courses at least 6 credits </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#84:1> 4. Other courses ( open) up to 12 credits </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#85:1> Part-time Level I students should register for the following

<p> up to 24 credits ( 9 credits </p>

<p> maximum in Social Sciences) </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#87:1> Students with lower level matriculation: </p>

<p> - with 1 A-Level: up to 21 credits

<p> - with no A-Levels up to 15 credits </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#88:1> Part-time students, too, must observe the order of priority

<ICE-JA:W2D-008#90:1> Students registering for this course can taken no more than <bold> 24

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#91:1> ( f) A student may, <bold> with the permission of the Dean,

change Majors. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#92:1> Full-time students must do this no later than the start of their

third semester of registration.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#93:1> Part-time students who have registered in accordance with regulation

9 ( b) below, must do it no later than the start of the fifth semester of

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#94:1> <h> <bold> FULL-TIME PROGRAMME </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#95:1> 8. ( a) A full-time student shall complete the degree programme

in not less than six and not more than twelve semesters. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#96:1> ( b) A full-time student should normally register for not more

than fifteen and not less than twelve credits per semester, except in the

case of the final two semesters of the degree programme in which a student

may seek permission to register for a maximum of 42 credits ( including the

Level III University course but nor a substitute) to complete the degree.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#97:1> ( c) A student will be required to withdraw from the Faculty

unless he or she has gained at least: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#98:1> Twelve credits by the end of the second semester </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#99:1> Twenty-four credits by the end of the fourth semester </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#100:1> Thirty-nine credits by the end of the sixth semester </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#101:1> Fifty-seven credits by the end of the eighth semester </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#102:1> Seventy-five credits by the end of the tenth semester </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#103:1> Ninety credits by the end of the twelfth semester. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#104:1> <h> <bold> PART-TIME PROGRAMME </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#105:1> 9. ( a) A part-time student shall complete the degree in not

less than eight and not more than eighteen semesters. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#106:1> ( b) Normally, a part-time student will be required to spread

the Level I programme over four semesters.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#107:1> The Level I Univer <l> sity course <bold> must </bold> be done in

the first semester. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#108:1> ( c) A part-time student will not normally be permitted to

register for Level II courses unless he or she has gained at least eighteen

Level I credits. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#109:1> ( d) A part-time student who has gained eighteen or more Level I

credits must take at least 12 and not more than 24 credits in each subsequent

year, except that a student who requires fewer than 12 credits to complete

the requirements for the degree need take only those credits.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#110:1> Permission may be sought to read for up to 30 credits to complete

the requirements of the degree, provided that UC3XO and not a substitute is

included among these 30. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#111:1> ( e) A part-time student will be required to withdraw from the

Faculty unless he or she has gained at least: </p>

<p> Six credits by the end of the second semester </p>

<p> Twelve credits by the end of the fourth semester </p>

<p> Twenty-one credits by the end of the sixth semester </p>

<p> Thirty credits by the end of the eighth semester </p>

<p> Forty-two credits by the end of the tenth semester </p>

<p> Fifty-four credits by the end of the twelfth semester </p>

<p> Sixty-six credits by the end of the fourteenth semester </p>

<p> Seventy-eight credits by the end of the sixteenth semester </p>

<p> Ninety credits by the end of the eighteenth semester </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#112:1> <h> <bold> TRANSFERS </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#113:1> All students wishing to transfer must fill out Transfer Forms at

the Student Affairs office in the Registry by 31st March. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#114:1> <bold> A. </bold> Transfer from Part-time to </p>

<p> Full-time Status and Vice Versa </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#115:1> ( i) Part-Time students with one A-Level who have not passed 21

credits after one year of study but who request a transfer to Full-Time

status must trade the 6 credits for Matricula <l> tion purposes and will not

receive these credits towards the degree. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#116:1> ( ii) The traded course may count as satisfying the

pre-requisite for entry to another course where it is required. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#117:1> ( iii) Part-Time students who have passed 21 credits at Level I

in their first year of study and who wish to transfer to Full-Time status

will not be required to trade any of these credits. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#118:1> <bold> B. </bold> Transfer to the Faculty of Arts and General

Studies from Other Faculties or Other Tertiary Institutions </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#119:1> ( i) Students transferring into the Faculty may request credit

for, or exemption from, courses completed in another Faculty or Institution.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#120:1> They should apply in writing to the Dean specifying the course(s),

the period of study, and the level obtained.
<ICE-JA:W2D-008#121:1> They may be provisionally registered pending consideration of their

request. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#122:1> ( ii) Exemptions and credits are not normally granted in respect

of Level II and Level III courses, except for the Level II University Course.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-008#123:1> ( iii) Credit will not be given to a transfer student for passes

obtained for Faculty courses in the Preliminary Year in the Faculty of

Natural Sciences. </p>
