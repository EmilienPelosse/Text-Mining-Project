<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#5:1> <h> <bold> Abstract </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#6:1> A method for the determination of salbutamol in both tablets and

syrups is described.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#7:1> It utilizes the reduction of the Folin-Ciocalteau reagent by the

phenolic group, monitoring the absorbance of the resulting complex at 760 nm.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#8:1> Results obtained are linear over the range 0-6 &mu; g ml <sp> - 1

</sp> salbutamol.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#9:1> Coloring material was removed by anionexchange chromatography prior to

analysis and there was no interference from sucrose, neutral flavorings or

the common preservative sodium benzoate.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#10:1> This method appears suitable as a general assay for salbutamol. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#11:1> <h> <bold> 1. Introduction </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#12:1> Salbutamol, <bold> 1 </bold> , &alpha; - [ ( <it> tert </it> -

butylamino)methyl]-4-hydroxy- <it> m </it> - xylene- &alpha; , &alpha; <it> '

</it> - diol, the structure of which is shown in Fig. 1, is a sympathomimetic

amine containing a phenolic group.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#13:1> Salbutamol sulfate in various forms  aerosol spray, tablets, syrups,

injection  is extensively used in the treatment of asthma. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#14:1> The recent expiration of the proprietary rights of the original

patent holders has led to the widespread utilization of the generic sub <l>

colored to appeal to specific local tastes. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#15:1> The most recent official B.P. [ 1] and U.S.P. [ 2] assays for

salbutamol ( as the sulfate) in formulation are based on HPLC and non-aqueous

and aerosol sprays.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#16:1> Although the non-aqueous titration method is straightforward, it is

inapplicable to syrups and solutions, while the other methods are unsuitable

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#17:1> An earlier B.P. method [ 3] is based on anion-exchange chromatography

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#18:1> Although suitable to relatively low-technology environments and, in

principle, ap <l> plicable to a number of formulations, this method is

described specifically for tablets only; it is subject to interference from

neutral and cationic excipients, and additives, several of which absorb in

the same mid-UV region as salbutamol. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#19:1> Recently published salbutamol assays in <l> clude a

microtitrimetric method [ 4], described in application for tablets only, and

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#88:2> Ribosomal DNA ( rDNA) from rice ( <it> Oriza Sativa </it> ) was used

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#90:2> The polymerase chain reaction was also used to amplify genomic DNA of

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#91:2> The amplification patterns of <it> D rotundata-cayenensis </it> cv

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#92:2> The results showed polymorphisms among the different yam cultivars.

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#95:2> <h> <bold> INTRODUCTION </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-037#96:2> Yam ( <it> Dioscorea </it> sp) are monocots belonging to the <it>

Dio <l> scorea </it> genus which contains about 600 species growing mainly in

the tropics and subtropics ( Coursey 1967).
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#97:2> Only a few species are edible and constitute and impor <l> tant

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#98:2> In Jamaica, six species containing approximately 12 cultivars are

widely grown.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#99:2> It has been shown that yam tubers are biochemically diverse in terms

enzyme activities ( Muzac-Tucker <it> et al </it> 1993).
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#100:2> The taxonomy of yam cultivars is complicated by the lack of clear

morphological differ <l> ences between closely related species.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#101:2> With the advent of molecular techniques, DNA-based approaches have

been proposed to generate genetic fingerprinting of numerous organisms (

</it> 1991).
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#102:2> One common pro <l> cedure has been DNA restriction fragment length

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#103:2> RFLP are genetic markers based on cloned molecules of DNA.
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#104:2> Using RFLP, scientists can construct high-density genetic linkage

maps and locate economically important genes ( Young 1992).
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#105:2> Williams <it> et al </it> ( 1990) reported a novel DNA analysis

technique based on the amplification of random DNA sequences using the

polymerase chain reaction ( PCR) and single primers of arbitrary sequence,

called random amplified polymorphic DNA ( RAPD).
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#106:2> This new technology is currently used in numerous organisms to

construct genetic maps, identify important genes and for popu <l> lation

genetic studies ( Waugh and Powell 1992).
<ICE-JA:W2A-037#107:2> However, such information on DNA fingerprinting or genetic markers

<ICE-JA:W2A-037#108:2> In this study, 12 cultivars of yam species were identified for their
