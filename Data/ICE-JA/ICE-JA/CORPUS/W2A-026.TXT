<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-026#116:1> <h> Discussion </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-026#117:1> The reduction of chromate by ascorbate occurs <it> via </it> the

formation of a chromate-ascorbate ester intermediate in the rate-determining

step.
<ICE-JA:W2A-026#118:1> This intermediate undergoes rapid electron transfers, <it> via </it>

an inner-sphere type mechanism, to produce chromium( <smallcaps> III

</smallcaps> ) and dehydroascorbate as final products. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-026#119:1> The estimate of the equilibrium constant, <it> K </it> <sb> e

</sb> , for the chromate-ascorbate ester formation ( 740-1000 dm <sp> 3 </sp>

mol <sp> - 1 </sp> ) , obtained in our study are similar to that reported (

K= 250 dm <sp> 3 </sp> mol <sp> - 1 </sp> ) for the formation of the

oxygen-bonded chromate <?> - </?> isopropyl alcohol ester. <fnr> <sp> 23a

</sp> </fnr> </p>
