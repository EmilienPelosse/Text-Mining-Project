says Carl Rattray </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#2:1> By Gary Spaulding </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#3:1> MEMBERS of the Joint Select Committee which met to discuss the "

tussle over whether there is a rationale in linking a weapon to an offence.

</p>

<p> <quote>
<ICE-JA:W2C-003#4:1> " The Gun Court legislation in my view has been a dismal failure

jurisprudentially and factually.
<ICE-JA:W2C-003#5:1> I will state it on any platform, any place.
<ICE-JA:W2C-003#6:1> Because the concept of attaching the offence to the weapon is a wrong

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#7:1> Comprised of members of the House of Representatives and the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#8:1> Mr. Rattray, who evidently felt strongly about the issue, was

<ICE-JA:W2C-003#9:1> " In my view there is absolutely no rationale in making a connection

<ICE-JA:W2C-003#10:1> There is absolutely none at all." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#11:1> Referring to recommendations made by a committee headed by the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#15:1> <h> Wrongly followed </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#16:1> At this point, chairman of the Select Committee, K.D. Knight,

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#17:1> However, Mr. Rattray ventured a reply. <quote>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#20:1> Mr. Knight: <quote> " Did the Gun Court attach an offence to the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#23:1> Mr. Rattray: <quote> " The Gun Court created a system of

<ICE-JA:W2C-003#24:1> It abolished in that court the right to trial by jury except where it

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#25:1> He ventured a prediction that <quote> " any attempt to make the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#26:1> Opposition member Bruce Golding, supporting Mr. Rattray's view,

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#27:1> Mr. Knight countered: <quote> " A man who has acquired an M16,

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#29:1> Mr. Golding: <quote> " No, but he might have." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#30:1> Mr. Knight: <quote> " In one instance he must have had and in the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-003#31:1> Mr. Golding: <quote> " With respect Sir, I am not a lawyer but if
