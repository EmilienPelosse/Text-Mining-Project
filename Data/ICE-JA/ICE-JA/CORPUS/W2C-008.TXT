<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X52:3> <h> For Roaring River areas- </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X53:3> <h> Major moves to combat typhoid </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X54:3> Prime Minister P.J. Patterson says he is not prepared to have

the health of communities put at risk because persons are not performing

their duties properly. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X55:3> He has urged residents of typhoid hit Westmoreland to practice

proper hygiene and not to do anything that would contaminate their water

supply. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X56:3> Mr. Patterson was speaking on Tuesday during a tour of the

affected communities.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X57:3> He was accompanied by Health Minister, Easton Douglas, Minister of

State in the Ministry of Public Utilities, Transport and Energy, Bobby Jones,

and officials from the National Water Commission ( NWC). </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X58:3> The Prime Minister announced that US$1.6 million had been

identified to undertake major corrective and preventative work on Roaring

River water supply system. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X59:3> The Roaring River Project, which is funded by the United Nations

Development Programme ( UNDP), the United States Agency for International

Development ( USAID), and the Government of Jamaica, will be carried out in

three phases. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X60:3> Work will include the safeguarding of pipelines that lead from

Roaring River into the communities.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X61:3> This is to ensure that there is no breaking of the lines so that

once the water is treated, it is safe and not subjected to further

contamination. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X62:3> The pipes will also be lifted to prevent breakage and the

resulting contamination of water when debris filters in. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X63:3> Steps will be taken to ensure that the treatment of water is

adequate and reliable.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X64:3> In addition to this, Mr. Patterson announced that he had given

instructions that capable persons should be put in charge of the water plant.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X65:3> The Prime Minister announced that allocations had been made to

have 1,000 pit latrines built.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X66:3> These will be subjected to conditions which will prevent seepage

into the underground water sources.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X67:3> Work has already begun on 122 of these latrines. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X68:3> He highlighted the need for the relevant agencies to work

together in solving the problem.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X69:3> To this end, the Ministry of Health, the National Water Commission

and the Jamaica Attractions Development Company will be collaborating on the

Roaring River Project. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X70:3> He appealed to the residents of the communities to play their

part by maintaining proper hygienic standards, and by not doing anything to

contaminate the water supply.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X71:3> He asked them to co-operate with the health educators and community

health aides who were working in the communities. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X72:3> He assured the residents that the plans to develop Roaring River

into a tourism theme park would not be abandoned.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X73:3> However, typhoid outbreak would have to be brought under control to

avoid putting the project at risk. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X74:3> After touring Roaring River, the Prime Minister visited the

Savanna-la-mar General Hospital where he visited some of the patients,

including the confirmed victims of the typhoid outbreak, and thanked the

health officials and personnel for working diligently in bringing the disease

under control. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2C-008#X75:3> Mr. Patterson's tour of the affected areas followed one by the

opposition Jamaica Labour Party last week.
<ICE-JA:W2C-008#X76:3> A statement issued by the JLP after the tour had made a number of

suggestions including a government funded programme to build safe pit

latrines. </p> </I>
