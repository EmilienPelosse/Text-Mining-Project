<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#21:2> <bold> DATE:</bold> April 11, 2001</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#22:2> <bold> RE: MONA COMMONS SQUATTER SETTLEMENT</bold></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#23:2> In light of the strong reservations which have come to the fore

regarding the proposed relocation of the Mona Commons Squatter Community, we

are seeking your support in requesting that the University Council <bold>

rescind the F&GPC decision of October 30, 2000 and set up a sub-committee to

explain to the Government of Jamaica why the University is unable to accede

to its request.</bold></p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#24:2> Should the Council be unwilling to take such a position, we are

requesting that at the very least the Council place on hold the October 30,

F&GPC decision and designate a sub-committee to enter into dialogue with the

Jamaican Government with a view to resolving the problems of the Mona Commons

Settlement, without implementing the proposed Housing Scheme for UWI lands,

and that discussions also be held with the Sir John Golding rehabilitation

Centre with a view to addressing their concerns without recourse to further

action.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#25:2> Please find attached a statement that outlines the basis for our

reservations.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#26:2> We look forward to your cooperation on this matter.</p></I>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#27:3> April 4, 2001</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#28:3> Professor Hubert Devonish</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#29:3> Academic Board Representative</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#30:3> Department of Language, Linguistics & Philosophy</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#31:3> UWI, MONA</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#32:3> Dear Professor Devonish,</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#33:3> On October 13, 2000 the University F& GPC ( min. 82 and 86) gave

its approval to the Jamaican Government's proposal to relocate the Mona

Commons squatter settlement to 3.2 acres of University lands adjacent to the

Mona Rehabilitation Centre and the site where the University laundry is

located.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#34:3> The members of WIGUT are requesting that this decision be

reviewed and revoked based on the following:</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#35:3> 1.<space> The UWI holds lands in trust for the people of the

Caribbean in keeping with the aims of the Charter ( 1998, Volume I):</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#36:3> 3. ( t)<space> <italics> To act as trustees or managers of any

property, legacy, endowment, bequest or gift <bold> for purposes of education

or research or otherwise in the furtherance of the work and welfare of the

University</bold></italics> &dotted- line;</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#37:3> We are firmly of the view that no more UWI land should be taken

over for any but educational purposes.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#38:3> What seems to be a great deal of land today will appear like nothing

in 50 years time.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#39:3> This in light of the fact that nearly every permanent structure

between Matilda's Corner and August Town, the area that encompasses the Mona

campus, has been erected in the last 55 years.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#40:3> 2.<space> The project is unsuitable for the relocation of the

community given its design and the nature of the family structure of the

average Mona Commons household.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#41:3> In addition there is strong evidence that the persons for whom it is

supposed to be designed consider the proposed housing units unsuitable.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#42:3> It would not be long before the three-storied one bedroom and studio

apartments become nothing more than an over crowded high-rise slum as has

happened with a number of others in Kingston.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#43:3> Professor Hubert Devonish</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#44:3> Academic Board Representative</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#45:3> Page 2</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#46:3> April 4, 2001</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#47:3> 3.<space> Neither the proposed housing scheme, nor the UWI lands

identified to accommodate it, can house the entire community of Mona Commons.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#48:3> In the past, there have been moves to resettle the community.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#49:3> These however have only served to relocate a part of the community

thus leaving the remainder to regenerate itself.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#50:3> There is nothing to suggest the entire community will be moved at

this time, in which case nothing will be gained from the move.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#51:3> 4.<space> The realities of Jamaican politics are such that

government housing has been used to develop a plethora of garrison

communities.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#52:3> These have become havens for high levels of crime and electoral

manipulation that have impacted negatively on neighboring communities.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#53:3> A professional analysis of the nature of the Mona Commons community

clearly shows the symptoms of a garrison in formation.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#54:3> What the UWI has done in effect is agree to have built on its own

lands a scheme that shows all the signs of becoming an institutionalized

garrison.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#55:3> 5.<space> In bringing together some of the most promising young

people of the Caribbean at Mona we believe that the Council should have

foremost in its mind their security.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#56:3> Already this issue has been a major concern at Mona.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#57:3> While recognizing that it is impossible to insulate the Campus from

developments taking place in the wider community, we cannot accept a

decision, in the community's best interest, that is likely to undermine the

security of our brightest sons and daughters or place them in an environment

in which they would be adversely affected.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#58:3> The decision taken by the University F&GPC is an unfortunate one.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#59:3> It seems to have been done without sufficient time for professional

reflection on all the ramifications.
<ICE-JA:W1B-020#60:3> We are therefore calling on the University Council to rescind the

decision.</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#61:3> Yours sincerely,</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#62:3> <@> Andrea Gilbert-Stephenson</@>, PhD</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W1B-020#63:3> President  WIGUT Jamaica</p></I>
