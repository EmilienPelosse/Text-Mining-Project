<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#1:1> <h> <bold> Jamaican Standard Specification for </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#2:1> <h> <bold> 1. Scope </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#3:1> This standard specifies quality requirements for Ficus abijan and

Ficus robusta to be supplied to the consumer.
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#4:1> It defines the quality requirements at the dispatching stage after

preparation and packing. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#5:1> <h> <bold> 2. Definition </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#6:1> For the purpose of this standard the following definition applies:

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#7:1> <bold> 2.1 fresh. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#8:1> Turgid, brightly coloured with a natural sheen. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#9:1> <h> <bold> 3. Quality requirements </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#10:1> <bold> 3.1 </bold> The produce shall have reached an appropriate

stage of growth and shall have been carefully harvested.
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#11:1> It shall be turgid and brightly coloured with a natural sheen.
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#12:1> It shall be free from: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#13:1> ( a)all pests, disease and damage caused therefrom; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#18:1> <bold> 3.2 </bold> The leaves shall have the colour typical of

the variety and shall be whole. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#19:1> <bold> 3.3 </bold> There shall be a well-developed root system as

evidenced by a creamy white colour and a minimum of 3.5 mm root growth. </p>

<ICE-JA:W2D-003#185:4> <h> <bold> Jamaican Standard for </p>

<p> Cosmetics: Part 4: Microbiological test methods </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#186:4> <h> <bold> 1. Scope </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#187:4> This standard specifies reference methods for the qualitative

and quantitative detection of micro-organisms in cosmetic products.
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#188:4> Since the presence of some micro-organisms in cosmetic preparations

constitutes a significant health hazard to the consumer, it is important that

effect <l> tive standard methods for the microbial analysis of cosmetics be

used.
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#189:4> These methods are not considered final but are thought to be the

most reliable at present. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#190:4> <h> <bold> 2. Definitions </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#191:4> For the purpose of this standard the following definitions

apply: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#192:4> <bold> 2.1 aerobic micro-organisms. <bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#193:4> Micro-organisms ( bacteria, yeast and moulds) which grow only in the

presence of free oxygen when tested in accordance with the methods specified.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#194:4> <bold> 2.2 total aerobic plate count. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#195:4> The number of aerobic micro-organisms found per millilitre or gram

of cosmetic product, when tested in accordance with the methods specified (

see <bold> 7.4.2 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#196:4> <bold> 2.3 staphylococcus aureus. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#197:4> Micro-organisms which form typical colonies on the surface of a

selective culture medium and show a positive coagulase reaction when tested

according to the method specified ( see <bold> 7.4.2.4 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#198:4> <bold> 2.4 staphylococcus aureus plate count. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#199:4> The number of Staphylococcus aureus found per milli <l> litre or

gram of cosmetic product when tested according to the method specified ( see

<bold> 7.4.3 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#200:4> <bold> 2.5 yeast and mould plate count. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#201:4> The number of yeast and mould per millilitre or gram of cosmetic

product when tested according to the method specified ( see <bold> 7.4.4

</bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#202:4> <bold> 2.6 sterility test. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#203:4> Test suitable for revealing the presence of viable forms of

bacteria, fungi and yeast in a cosmetic product ( see <bold> 7.4.7 </bold> ) .

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#204:4> <h> <bold> 3. Principle </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#205:4> <bold> 3.1 Determination of total bacterial load of cosmetic

products. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#206:4> Use the recommended total aerobic plate count method ( see <bold>

7.4.2 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#207:4> <bold> 3.2 Detection of typical colonies of Staphylococcus

aureus. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#208:4> Use the recommended selective media; select and subject typical

colonies to confirmation tests for coagulase activity; cal <l> culate the

number of Staphylococcus aureus per millilitre or gram of cosmetic product

from the number of confirmed typical colonies ( see <bold> 7.4.3.3 </bold> to

<bold> 7.4.3.5 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#209:4> <bold> 3.3 Detection of yeasts and moulds in the cosmetic

products. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#210:4> Use the recommended selective media.
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#211:4> Calculate the number of yeast and moulds per millilitre or gram of

cosmetic product ( see <bold> 7.4.4 </bold> ) . </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-003#212:4> <bold> 3.4 Sterility testing. </bold>
<ICE-JA:W2D-003#213:4> Detection of viable micro- organisms in the cosmetic product using

the recommended enrichment broth.
