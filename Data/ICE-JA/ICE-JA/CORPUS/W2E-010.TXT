<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#49:2> <h> Tourism Report paints gloomy picture </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#50:2> THE first person to object to my column <quote> " Jamaica - last

place to take a vacation" </quote> was Robert Stephens, the Director of

Tourism.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#51:2> This was most disingenuous of him, since he was sitting on top of an

official assessment of the Jamaican tourism product which was far worse than

mine. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#52:2> In 1990 the Jamaican Government commissioned Shirley Fletcher to

survey and report on the state of the tourism product.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#53:2> Over 24 days, 146 persons in Kingston, Port Antonio, Ocho Rios,

Montego Bay and Negril contributed views, perspectives and statistical data,

and 12 project documents were considered. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#54:2> The report found that Government had <quote> " &dotted-line;no

clearly articulated vision for tourism development," </quote> and that

<quote> " ( hotel) accommodation has outstripped the provision of

infrastructure services, in particular, sewage and telephone" </quote> .
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#55:2> It also found that people from all over the island had descended in

their tens of thousands on the <quote> " boom" </quote> towns in search of

employment.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#56:2> This has led to widespread squatting, and the <quote> " growth of an

informal labour market primarily consisting of legal and illegal

goods&dotted-line; with the result that the very prosperity that attracted

the poor is now threatened by them." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#57:2> There are over 6,000 squatters in Ocho Rios. Squatters on the

Belmont Property above Dunn's River Falls use the river as a sanitary

facility.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#58:2> The White River is polluted with fecal matter yet tourist rafting

continues there. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#59:2> Hotels in Negril and Montego Bay dump their raw sewage over the

fence.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#60:2> Pipes connected to toilets in tourist villas in the West End of

Negril empty directly into the sea.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#61:2> The location and nature of the sewage disposal system erected by the

Urban Development Corporation at Seacastles has <quote> "

&dotted-line;infuriated local residents who have termed the system 'Doodo

Park'." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#62:2> Thousands of <quote> " economic refugees" </quote> have squatted

behind the new police station in Negril.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#63:2> There are no medical facilities in the resort, and public sanitary

facilities range from inadequate to non-existent.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#64:2> The result of squatting in north coast resort towns has been rising

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#66:2> The misrepresentation of tours such as the Brimmer Hall

<ICE-JA:W2E-010#67:2> The tours are cut by as much as 50% as tour operators rush back to

<ICE-JA:W2E-010#68:2> The Report also describes the slow processing of tourists at our two

<ICE-JA:W2E-010#69:2> Even the Water Sports Operations are in the business of hiring touts

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#70:2> It also refers to a payola system in Ocho Rios where Indian In

Bond merchants pay a fee per head to taxi-drivers for each passenger coming

off a cruise ship who visits their stores.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#71:2> It says that craft vendors and other shop owners harass the few

<ICE-JA:W2E-010#72:2> There are seven craft markets in Ocho Rios with 600 vendors, and 13

<ICE-JA:W2E-010#73:2> There is little social infrastructure to support this.
<ICE-JA:W2E-010#74:2> Housing and hospital services are inadequate; there is poor sewage

<p> <ICE-JA:W2E-010#76:2> In August 1990 there were 50 hotels on the northcoast, all
