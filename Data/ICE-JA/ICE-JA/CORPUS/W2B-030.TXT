<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-030#1:1> <h> <bold> How much of the B Vitamins do we need? </bold> </h>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-030#2:1> The amount of the B Vitamins we need vary for each one.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#3:1> In general, we need very small amount of each of these vitamins.

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-030#5:1> The amounts of <}> <-> thiamin </-> <+> thiamine </+> </}> ( B1),

<ICE-JA:W2B-030#6:1> Our requirements for them are therefore directly linked to our energy

( calories) intake.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#7:1> The amounts needed vary between 0.5-1 milligram per 1000 calories.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#8:1> Niacin and vitamin B6 requirements are also related to an adequate

intake of high quality protein.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#9:1> Proteins of animal origin ( except gelatin) and cereal/legume blends

offer protein of high quality. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-030#10:1> The needs for these vitamins rise rapidly during the growing

years.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#11:1> Teenagers are very active and need more than adults.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#12:1> An adult male ingesting about 3,000 calories would need 1.5

<ICE-JA:W2B-030#13:1> He needs about 2 milligrams of B6, and 2 micrograms each of folacin

<ICE-JA:W2B-030#14:1> The term pre-formed simply means that niacin, already in the food can

<ICE-JA:W2B-030#15:1> The body can also make niacin from the amino acid tryptophan. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-030#16:1> Note however, that when you look at a table of Recommended

<ICE-JA:W2B-030#17:1> Also the adult male is taken as the reference individual as his

calorie intake and hence vitamin requirements are greatest.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#18:1> The active, fast developing teenager would require similar amounts.
<ICE-JA:W2B-030#19:1> There is also an extra demand for the B vitamins during pregnancy and
