<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#57:1> <h> <bold> NATIONAL PARKS TRUST FUND </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#58:1> The objective of the National Parks Trust Fund is to finance the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#65:1> <h> <bold> NATIONAL PARKS LEGISLATION </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#66:1> Existing legislation including the Beach Control Act of 1956 and

<ICE-JA:W2B-027#67:1> However, these laws are outdated, while enforcement under them is lax

<ICE-JA:W2B-027#68:1> There <l> fore, there is a need for new legisla <l> tion which deals

<ICE-JA:W2B-027#69:1> The recently passed NRCA-Act is being carefully exam <l> ined for its

suitability to encompass provisions for national parks and protected areas.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#70:1> Regulations for national parks and specific Orders relating to the

Government, and hopefully they will be passed into law very soon. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#71:1> <h> <bold> CONCLUSIONS </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#72:1> The project has been in progress for some time and it is

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#79:1> There is another major difference between the PARC Project and

<ICE-JA:W2B-027#81:1> The first three are achievable within a specific time frame; the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#84:1> The PARC Project is a new beginning.

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#86:2> <h> <bold> Montego Bay Marine Park: Protecting a Vital Resource

</bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#87:2> Montego Bay is the largest tourist resort in Jamaica and the

island's second city.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#88:2> About half of the one million tourists visiting Jamaica each year

stay in and around Montego Bay.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#89:2> They generate an estimated one-third of the US$600 million which

Jamaica earns annually from tourism.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#90:2> Montego Bay's attraction as a tourist desti <l> nation lies in its

six beaches, clean coastal waters, coral reefs and other marine life.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#91:2> The product being marketed is a beautiful, natural environment.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#92:2> This is just one reason why a marine park in Montego Bay is of vital

impor <l> tance. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#93:2> <h> <bold> HISTORY </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#94:2> The first marine park of the island was initiated by the Jamaica

Tourist Board; a 59- hectare area was declared protected under the Beach

Control Act ( Beach Control Order 1974).
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#95:2> Regulations to govern activities in the park were approved by the

Ministry of Mining and Tourism.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#96:2> At the time, the marine park project was viewed by some with

expectation and optimism.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#97:2> It was felt that where previous attempts for an Ocho Rios Marine Park

had failed for lack of funds, the Cornwall Beach Marine Park would succeed

under the management of the Jamaica Tourist Board.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#98:2> Unfortu <l> nately, these expectations were not met: legislation was

never enforced and most people soon forgot that a park had been declared at

all.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#99:2> Yet the exercise proved to be of some value in the end: the

regulations now form the legal basis on which the new and expanded Montego

Bay Marine Park will be established. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#100:2> Funded by the Organization of American States, a Marine Parks

Action Committee was set up in 1986.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#101:2> Management, funding and administrative needs for an opera <l> tional

Marine National Parks System were evaluated by O'Callaghan, Woodley and Aiken

( 1988).
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#102:2> Their timely report integrated recommen <l> dations on the

establishment of marine protected areas given by the Jamaica Country

Environmental Profile ( CEP 1987), a project funded by USAID.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#103:2> This inspired USAID's commitment to the establishment of a National

Parks System in Jamaica, and to fund what is now the Pro <l> tected Areas

Resource Conservation ( PARC) Project.
<ICE-JA:W2B-027#104:2> The Montego Bay Marine Park is one component of this venture. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#105:2> <h> <bold> OBJECTIVES </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#106:2> The objectives of the Montego Bay Marine Park are to: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#107:2> 1. Outline the Park boundaries. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#109:2> 3. Set up corps of Park Rangers who will enforce regulations

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#110:2> 4. Educate the community about the aims of the Park, its

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#111:2> 5. Influence development outside the Park boundaries so that the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#112:2> 6. Review current conditions in the Park at the start of the pro

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#113:2> 7. Facilitate the recovery of fish populations and benthic

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-027#114:2> 8. Develop a comprehensive man <l> agement plan addressing the
