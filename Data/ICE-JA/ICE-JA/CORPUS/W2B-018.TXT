<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#2:2> <bold> THE PEOPLE </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#3:2> Jamaica's multi-racial population of approximately 2.5 million

primarily comprises people of African, European, East Indian and Chinese

origin.
<ICE-JA:W2B-018#4:2> Although English is the official lan <l> guage, most people speak an

English- based Creole  patois. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#5:2> Originally populated by Tainos, the country was claimed for Spain

by the explorer Christopher Columbus in 1494.
<ICE-JA:W2B-018#6:2> In 1655 Jamaica was captured from the Spaniards by the British until

1962, when the country became an independent member of the British

Commonwealth of Nations. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#7:2> <h> <bold> GOVERNMENT </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#8:2> Jamaica is a constitutional monarchy and as a member of the

British Commonwealth, the Queen of England, Elizabeth II is the titular head

of the country.
<ICE-JA:W2B-018#9:2> She is repre <l> sented in Jamaica by a Governor General. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#10:2> The Jamaican Parliament is bicamer <l> al, that is, it consists

Represent <l> atives, also know as the Lower House. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#11:2> The members of the House of Representatives are elected under

<ICE-JA:W2B-018#12:2> There are 60 constituencies, each represented by one Member of

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#13:2> The Senate comprises twenty-one members appointed by the Governor

<ICE-JA:W2B-018#14:2> The Senate functions mainly as a review chamber for legis <l> lation

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#15:2> The Cabinet is the principal instru <l> ment of government

<ICE-JA:W2B-018#18:2> The Minister of Finance must be an elected mem <l> ber of the House

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#46:2> <h> <bold> HOUSING </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#47:2> Both public and private sectors are engaged in the provision of

housing in Jamaica. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#48:2> In 1975 the Government established the National Housing

Corporation ( NHC) and National Housing Trust ( NHT) to acceler <l> ate

housing development. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#49:2> ( NHT) is funded by a combination of personal and corporate

contributions through salary deductions.
<ICE-JA:W2B-018#50:2> The NHT Act requires that all employees over eighteen years, and

their employers, make contributions to the Trust.
<ICE-JA:W2B-018#51:2> Self-employed persons are also required to make contributions. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#52:2> Employers are required to deduct 2 per cent of each employee's

salary along with 3 per cent of their own wage bill and remit funds to the

NHT on a monthly basis.
<ICE-JA:W2B-018#53:2> Self-employed persons contribute 2 per cent or 3 per cent of

earnings, depending on the occupational category in which they fall. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#54:2> These contributions are used by NHT to finance the construction

of houses and to provide mortgage loans for contributors who wish to

purchase, build, repair their houses or to pur <l> chase land. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#55:2> The Ministry of Environment and Housing, through its joint

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-018#98:2> The main impetus for economic growth has come from expansion in
