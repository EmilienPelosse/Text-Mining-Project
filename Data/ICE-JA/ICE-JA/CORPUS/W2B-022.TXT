<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#2:1> Choline is said to be a vitamin B complex vitamin.

<ICE-JA:W2B-022#5:1> Although made in the body through synthesis, it is not made in

<ICE-JA:W2B-022#6:1> It must therefore be supplied in food.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#7:1> Chole is the Greek word for bile and this explains the use of the name

<ICE-JA:W2B-022#8:1> It meets the requirements for classification as a vitamin and is

<ICE-JA:W2B-022#9:1> It <{> <-> retain </-> <+> retains </+> </{> its value despite

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#11:1> A choline deficiency may result in fatty livers with subsequent

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#14:1> <h> Folic Acid </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#15:1> Folic acid has been recognized as one of the B vitamins necessary

<ICE-JA:W2B-022#16:1> Its chemical formula is C <sb> 19 </sb> H <sb> 19 </sb> N <sb> 7

</sb> O <sb> 6 </sb> and it is slightly soluble in water and easily oxidized

intestinal tract to stay healthy.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#18:1> It has been indicated that up to three milligrams daily would be an

adequate amount.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#19:1> The bacteria in the intestines will ordinarily work to combine or

synthesize various elements together to form folic acid. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#20:1> A deficiency of this B vitamin may have serious results for the

expectant mother.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#21:1> The condition of a congenital fissure or split in the roof of the

mouth, known as cleft pate, may be the result of a deficiency in folic acid

substance.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#22:1> Also, although it is not certain, this deficiency may contribute to

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#23:1> Anemia is one of the conditions that may result from a deficiency

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#25:1> The condition known as spruce, where there is a sore mouth,

indigestion and diarrhoea may also results from the lack of folic acid.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#26:1> There may also result a shortage of the large red blood corpuscles,

the condition known as megaloblastic anemia. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#27:1> It is found abundantly in spinach, watercress and the greens of

mustard, beet turnips, parsley, carrots, broccoli and generally in almost all

green leaves of vegetables and herbs.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#28:1> It is also found in mushrooms, soybeans and wheat germ. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#41:1> <h> Carbohydrates  Inositol </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#52:1> <h> Lecithin </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#53:1> Lecithin is one of the derivatives of glycerin and is of value

<ICE-JA:W2B-022#58:1> It is also of value in relieving the angina pectoris pain in the

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-022#59:1> Heart disease resulting from arterosclerosis ( degeneration of

walls of the arteries) due to lack of sufficient lecithin to cope with a meal

that is high in fat or excessive in calories, with a condition of

arterosclerosis, gradually built up over a period of time, the fat particles

will often be unable to pass through the arterial walls and consequently

remain in the artries.
<ICE-JA:W2B-022#60:1> A vicious circle develops and as more fatty deposits form on the
