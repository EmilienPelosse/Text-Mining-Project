<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-027#4:1> ABSTRACT
<ICE-JA:W2A-027#5:1> A review of poisonings in Jamaica from 1980 to 1990 has been made

based on the analytical findings of the Toxicology Division of the Government

Chemist's Department of the Ministry of Health in Jamaica.
<ICE-JA:W2A-027#6:1> Samples were received from a number of sources as the Department

provided analytical toxicology services to Government agencies, private

institutions and physicians.
<ICE-JA:W2A-027#7:1> Pesticides were implicated in 16.5% of the positive human poisoning

samples and 65% of the positive animal poisoning samples.
<ICE-JA:W2A-027#8:1> In addition to modern synthetic drugs and pesticides, traditional

poisons from botanical sources were also encountered.
<ICE-JA:W2A-027#9:1> The authors feel that priority attention needs to be given to the

enforcement of legal safety provisions for storage, transportation, sale and

use of pesticides as their availability is likely to increase with efforts

for greater food production in Jamaica. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-027#52:1> <h> <bold> Lead Poisoning </bold> </h> </p>
