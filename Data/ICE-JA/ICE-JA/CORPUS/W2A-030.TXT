<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-030#103:1> <h> <bold> DISCUSSION </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2A-030#104:1> Ethylene synthesis can be regulated at two points in the

pathway, i.e., the conversion of methionine to ACC and the conversion of ACC

to ethylene.
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#105:1> Galactose was found to inhibit the production of ethylene by

affecting the conversion of ACC to ethylene.
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#106:1> This is evident from data presented in Table 3 and Figure 1.
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#107:1> During ripening the level of ACC increases <fnr> <sp> 17 </sp>

</fnr> , making it possible for an increase in the production of ethylene.

<footnote>
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#108:1> 17. Hoffman, N. E.; Yang, S. F. <it> J. Am. Soc. Hortic. </it> ,

1980, 105, 492. </footnote>
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#109:1> However, the production of ethylene falls considerably in fruits

that are overripe <fnr> <sp> 17 </sp> </fnr> .
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#110:1> This fall in ethylene production is not accom <l> panied by a fall

in the level of ACC <fnr> <sp> 7 </sp> </fnr> .
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#111:1> There <l> fore, the conversion of ACC to ethylene is likely to be

impaired in overripe fruits.
<ICE-JA:W2A-030#112:1> It is being suggested that the production of galac <l> tose by the

<ICE-JA:W2A-030#114:1> It could be that galactose alone does not con <l> tribute to the

&alpha;- and &beta;-glucosidase, &alpha;- and &beta;-mannosidase are also

involved in the regulation of ethylene synthesis by producing the

monosaccharides glucose and mannose respectively. </p>
