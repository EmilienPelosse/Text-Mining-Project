<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#1:1> <h> <bold> ENERGY POLICY </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#2:1> <h> <bold> Background </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#3:1> In recognition of the strategic importance of the energy sector to

the country's economic growth and development, an Energy Policy has been

developed.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#4:1> The Jamaican economy is not well endowed with petroleum based energy

resources and therefore exhibits a strong dependence on imports.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#5:1> In comparison with countries at a similar stage of development, the

level of energy requirement in Jamaica is fairly high.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#6:1> This can be attributed primarily to the requirements of the

bauxite/alumina industry which is highly energy intensive.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#7:1> The policy seeks to diversify Jamaica's energy base with the aim of

ensuring an adequate and secure energy supply for Jamaica. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#8:1> Within this Energy Policy, issues relating to energy sources such

as petroleum, renewables and other fuels are addressed.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#9:1> In keeping with the Government of Jamaica's commitment to deregulate

and liberalize the Jamaican economy, the involvement of the private sector on

a competitive basis is chosen as the best way to modernize and expand the

energy sector, so as to achieve the required growth in energy supplies and to

improve efficiencies in energy production. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#10:1> Based on the nation's projection of economic growth, it is

estimated that approximately 400 megawatts ( MW) of new generating capacity

will be required over the next ten ( 10) years in the electricity sector.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#11:1> This will offer opportunities for the participation of the private

sector.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#12:1> Presently, the system has a generating capacity of 694 MW. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#13:1> The petroleum sector, which satisfies approximately ninety ( 90%)

percent of the nation's energy needs, is supplied by Petrojam Limited, a

small hydro-skimming refinery and a network of international and local

marketing companies.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#14:1> The efficiency and capability of the refinery to produce some

products in adequate quantities is limited by its technology, size and age.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#15:1> If the refinery is to address these short-comings, it would have to

be modernized, upgraded or expanded.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#16:1> This can only be achieved through new investment, more than the

public sector can afford.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#17:1> Therefore, joint venture collaboration is needed in the petroleum

sector. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#18:1> <bold> 1. The objectives of the Energy Policy are to: </bold>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#19:1> a) ensure stable and adequate energy supplies at the least

economic cost in a deregulated and liberalized environment; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#20:1> b) diversify the energy base and encourage the develop <l> ment

of indigenous energy resources where economi <l> cally viable and technically

feasible; and ensure the security of energy supplies; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#21:1> c) encourage efficiency in energy production, conversion and use

with the overall objectives of reducing the energy intensity of the economy;

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#22:1> d) complement the country's Industrial Policy recogniz <l> ing

the importance of energy as a critical input to industrial growth and

stability; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#23:1> e) minimize the adverse environmental effects and pollution

caused by the production, storage, transport and use of energy, and minimize

environmental degradation as a result of the use of fuelwood; and </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#24:1> f) establish an appropriate regulatory framework to protect

consumers, investors and the environment. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#25:1> <bold> 2. These objectives will be achieved by creating an

enabling environment to: </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#26:1> a) encourage private sector participation and investments through

a policy of divestment and an appropriate regulatory framework conducive to

new investment; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#27:1> b) promote the development of indigenous energy sources where

appropriate, technically and economi <l> cally feasible; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#28:1> c) encourage energy conservation/efficiency on the supply side as

well as demand side management; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#29:1> d) fully protect the environment while ensuring that adequate

energy supplies are available to the country and to sustain the desired rate

of economic growth, and at the same time, stimulate industrial development by

encouraging synergies from co-generation; and </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#30:1> e) maintain appropriate institutional arrange <l> ments to ensure

that the stated objectives are achieved. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#31:1> <h> <bold> Power/ Electricity Sector </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#32:1> <bold> 3. </bold> Currently, all the generation, transmission and

distribution of electric power for public consumption are the direct

responsibility of the Government.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#33:1> GOJ has taken the decision to privatize and liberalize the

electricity sector over the long-term and as a first step, all new generating

capacity is being undertaken by the private sector ( inclusive of independent

self generators) through Independent Power Producers ( IPP).
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#34:1> This is a major policy initiative which is aimed at moving the system

of generation, transmission and distribution into largely private sector

ownership and management. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#35:1> A major policy initiative therefore in the power sector will be

to ensure that an appropriate regulatory framework under the auspices of the

Office of Utilities Regulation ( OUR) is put in place to: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#36:1> a) facilitate the timely provision of new power generating

capacity and the associated transmission works; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#37:1> b) establish an environment capable of attracting private

investments in the power sector and capable of supp <l> orting long term

financing for the private sector; and </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#38:1> c) prescribe the required standards in the power sector; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#39:1> d) ensure the safety of the consumers, the protection and

preservation of the environment in respect of utility services; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#40:1> e) generally support Jamaica's power sector privatization

programme. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#41:1> The energy policy for the power / electricity sector will ensure

that: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#42:1> a) appropriate regulatory oversight is put in place; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#43:1> b) the electricity rate setting mechanism reflects the true cost

of power; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#44:1> c) all new power generation projects are undertaken by the

private sector and the generation of electricity is done in an organized and

structured way; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#45:1> d) the use of co-generation to meet some portion of electricity

supply will continue to be pursued and principles under which the electricity

company purchases electricity from co-generators clearly established; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#46:1> e) the agency responsible for generation capacity planning will

prepare a periodic Least Cost Expansion Plan ( LCEP) for review, approval and

publication by the competent authority; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#47:1> f) the widespread publication of the indicative LCEP provides

guidance to potential private sector investors in the development of

proposals for new plants; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#48:1> g) the Rural Electrification Programme is continued in order to

maintain the extension of electricity to rural areas with a view to fostering

economic development and improving the quality of life in rural communities.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#49:1> <h> <bold> Petroleum Sector </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#50:1> <bold> 4. </bold> Currently, Jamaica imports petroleum and

petroleum products on favourable terms from Venezuela and Mexico under the

San Jos&eacute; Accord.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#51:1> As long as these contractual arrangements remain favourable from a

national interest point of view, they will be continued.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#52:1> Even with privatiza <l> tion, it is envisaged that the country will

continue to enjoy the benefits of crude oil supplies under the existing

contractual arrangements in the same way that Jamaica enjoyed these prior to

acquisition of the refinery in 1982.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#53:1> The market has been deregulated for the importation of all petroleum

products since 1993. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#54:1> <bold> 5. </bold> Government recognizes that the processing of

crude is a desirable option and as such, will encourage refinery operation in

the country.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#55:1> In this context, the Energy Policy will: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#56:1> a) encourage the local refining of petroleum through the granting

of an appropriate differential between tariffs on refinery feedstock and the

importation of fully refined petroleum products; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#57:1> b) encourage private sector participation in the refinery and

other government-owned petroleum/energy entities; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#58:1> c) review and optimize specifications for all petroleum products

to meet modern economic and environ <l> mental requirements; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#59:1> d) require importers and marketers of petroleum to supply timely

and relevant information regarding imports, sales, etc.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#60:1> The provision of this information will be necessary for proper energy

planning; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#61:1> e) review and establish the policy for the pricing of petroleum

products in order to achieve the objective of minimizing subsidies and

promoting the policy of energy conservation; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#62:1> f) ensure a predetermined level of inventory that will guarantee

that the nation's petroleum supplies are maintained at the required levels;

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#63:1> g) seek to maintain the protection of the environment and the

preservation/encouragement of natural and cultivated forests.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#64:1> Although for example, Government will cause the overall removal of

subsidy on domestic kerosene, it will remain committed to same direct subsidy

to assist those poor households that can be identified as totally dependent

on that petroleum product for cooking and lighting. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#65:1> <h> <bold> Development of Indigenous Energy Supplies </bold> </h>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#66:1> <bold> 6. </bold> The Government will continue to foster,

facilitate and encourage the development of all new and renewable energy

sources where feasible, but especially in the areas of: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#67:1> - hydropower </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#68:1> - biomass </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#69:1> - solar </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#70:1> - wind </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#71:1> <h> <bold> Energy Conservation/ Efficiency </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#72:1> <bold> 7. </bold> Since energy conservation and improved energy

efficiency appear to provide the greatest scope for reducing Jamaica's

requirements of imported energy, this aspect of the Energy Policy cannot be

over-emphasized.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#73:1> In this respect, the Energy Policy includes the following: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#74:1> a) the Government will support the dissemination of information

regarding energy conservation measures; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#75:1> b) all electricity generating entities will be encouraged to

continue the implementation of the loss reduction programmes; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#76:1> c) Demand Side Management ( DSM) programmes will be developed for

the power sector.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#77:1> The objectives of these programmes will be clearly specified and the

institutional arrangements are to be set out in DSM policy guidelines; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#78:1> d) Government will demonstrably support DSM programmes by

reducing or eliminating duties applicable to all energy efficient and energy

conservation devices and equipment; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#79:1> e) Government will ensure coordination between the transport

sector and energy planning; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#80:1> f) the appliance testing and labelling programme presently being

carried out at the Jamaica Bureau of Standards ( JBS) will be extended to all

appliances that use electric energy; </p>

<P> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#81:1> g) the Energy Efficiency Building Code ( EEBC) will be

promulgated and enforced; and </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#82:1> h) the Government will make it a legal requirement for industrial

boilers to be inspected annually. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#83:1> <h> bold> Energy and the Environment </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#84:1> <bold> 8. </bold> The protection of the environment is a primary

objective of this Energy Policy and as such, the environ <l> mental

guidelines of the Natural Resources Conservation Authority ( NRCA) relating

to the energy sector will be strictly enforced. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#85:1> <h> <bold> Institutional Arrangements </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#86:1> <bold> 9. </bold> a. The Ministry with responsibility for energy

will have overall responsibility for the implementation of the policy; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#87:1> b. The recently established Office of Utilities Regulation ( OUR)

will have oversight responsibility for the regula <l> tory framework for the

relevant energy sector entities.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#88:1> This office will protect the interest of both the consumer and the

investor in the provision and utilization of public utility services; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#89:1> c. The Ministry will foster the establishment of a central energy

conservation and renewable unit which will act as a facilitator for all new

and renewable energy projects.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#90:1> This unit will provide a central point for inputs from the public and

the scientific community with regard to technological developments etc.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#91:1> The unit will also provide information to the public. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#92:1> <h> <bold> Incentives </bold> </h> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#93:1> <bold> 10. </bold> As an incentive to the energy sector, the

following will be done: </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#94:1> - economic pricing will guide the pricing regime for all energy

supplies; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#95:1> - the tax to be applied on energy conservation equip <l> ment,

materials and supplies will be such that it will ensure that the items

involved are available to the public and that consumers will be encouraged to

invest in the most efficient end-use device or technology; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#96:1> - the use of solar panels and tubes for water heating, and solar

cells designed to produce electricity will be encouraged by maintain <l> ing

a concessionary tax structure on these items; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#97:1> - electric utilities will be provided with a mecha <l> nism to

recover the expenditure made for conservation programmes through the tariff

structure; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#98:1> - energy conservation represents a national saving to the economy

and as demonstrated through the Demand Side Management programme.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#99:1> It can also represent significant savings to industries.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#100:1> Government will encourage investors in energy conservation and DSM

to take advantage of the accelerated depreciation of capital equipment

schemes that now prevail; </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2B-031#101:1> - currently the electric utility allows up to 15% external

benefit for the development of renewable projects.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#102:1> That is, the utility is prepared to pay up to 15% more for

electricity generated by renewable sources compared to the average price paid

for fossil fuel generated electricity.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#103:1> This is to ensure that renewable sources of energy become a part of

the electricity generation mix.
<ICE-JA:W2B-031#104:1> In a privatized environment, this policy will be encouraged. </p>
