<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#34:1> <h> <bold> <ul> TEACHING WITH A DIFFERENCE </ul> </bold> </h>

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#35:1> <bold> Why Teaching With a Difference? </bold> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#36:1> a) To help the students to understand more easily and remember

things more readily. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#37:1> b) To capture and hold their attention while you are teaching

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#38:1> c) To add variety. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#39:1> We can remember anything and everything if we really want to: it

all depends on how important we think it is and also how it was presented to

us.
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#40:1> Sometimes some things are quite complicated but when properly

explained in the simplest terms and using everyday examples one can't help

remembering.
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#41:1> For example, students usually get the exercise to classify vitamins.

</p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#42:1> We usually teach - <quote> " Vitamins are divided into 2 groups -

water soluble and fat soluble.
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#43:1> The fat soluble vitamins are vitamins A or retinol; vitamin D or

sunshine vitamin; vitamin E or tocopherol and vitamin K or Anti-haemorragic

vitamin. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#44:1> The water soluble vitamins are B or thiamin, vitamin C or

ascorbic acid, vitamin B or riboflavin etc." </quote> </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#45:1> Gosh!
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#46:1> that is quite mouthful - what a confusion! </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#47:1> We could approach it differently.
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#48:1> We could tell a <quote> " Vitamin Story" </quote> using a tape

recorder. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#49:1> <quote> 'Once upon a time there was a man by the name of Dr.

Funk, he was responsible for the discovery of vitamins. </p>

<p> <ICE-JA:W2D-013#50:1> <quote> 'He now had to make a decision to differentiate between

those vitamins which could be <ul> dissolved </ul> in water and those which

could be <ul> dissolved </ul> only in fats, so he grouped them into 2 groups

based on the experiments he carried out.' </quote>
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#51:1> Now if you want to remember the fat soluble vitamins ( those which

can be dissolved in fat) just use the letters to form a person named <quote>

<bold> <ul> 'ADEK' </ul> </bold> </quote> .
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#52:1> Imagine this person as an obese or over-weight person - <ul> very fat

</ul> .
<ICE-JA:W2D-013#53:1> He/She represents the fat soluble vitamins - then all the other

vitamin would be water soluble - vitamin C and all the B's - <quote> <bold> 'Cold

Beaches' </bold> </quote> ( C and Bs which are associated with water). </p>
